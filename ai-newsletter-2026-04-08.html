Lead Story

Anthropic Hits $30B Revenue Run Rate — and Still Can't Keep Up With Demand

The company reported a 58% revenue surge in March alone, then immediately announced it lacks the compute capacity to serve everyone who wants in.

Anthropic's annualized revenue run rate has reached $30 billion — a figure that would have seemed implausible eighteen months ago and now looks almost conservative given the company's trajectory. The 58% single-month surge in March reflects a combination of Claude Opus 4.6's dominance on coding benchmarks and an explosion of enterprise API usage that the company openly admits it cannot fully satisfy.

To address the compute gap, Anthropic finalized an expanded partnership with Google and Broadcom to access data centers running Google's TPU chips. New capacity comes online in 2027 — which means the bottleneck is real and the timeline is not short. Google Cloud and Broadcom signed expanded chip agreements as part of the deal, signaling that the big compute constraint is now being treated as a shared infrastructure problem, not just Anthropic's alone.

Meanwhile, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing — a coalition of major tech and cybersecurity companies given early access to the unreleased Claude Mythos model, focused specifically on securing the world against AI-enabled cyber threats. It is a notable move: deploying an unreleased frontier model as a defensive security tool before it goes to the general public.

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Form a United Front Against Chinese Model Copying

The three largest US AI labs are sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum — the industry nonprofit they co-founded with Microsoft — specifically targeting adversarial distillation: Chinese competitors extracting outputs from US frontier models to train their own systems without licensing. The collaboration includes sharing detection methods for terms-of-service violations. It is a rare moment of cross-competitor cooperation on what all three companies consider an existential competitive threat.

New Research Cuts AI Energy Use by 100x — Without Sacrificing Accuracy

Researchers published results this week combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning — the first such hybrid to achieve up to a 100-fold reduction in energy consumption while simultaneously improving accuracy on reasoning tasks. The approach lets AI systems reason logically rather than relying on brute-force statistical pattern matching. If it scales to frontier models, it could fundamentally change the economics of inference — the single largest operating cost for AI companies at current volumes.

New Chip Operates at 700°C — Could Redefine AI Compute Limits

A new memory device built from ultra-durable materials can store data and perform calculations at 700°C (1,300°F) — far beyond the operating limits of any current silicon chip. The breakthrough, reported by ScienceDaily on April 6, could dramatically accelerate AI computation in environments where conventional chips fail: space, industrial sensors, and high-power AI accelerators that currently throttle under thermal load. Researchers say the material could eventually replace conventional memory in AI chips running at sustained maximum power.

Research from Google and Oratomic, published this week in Time, suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking current internet encryption standards may arrive sooner than previously projected — and that AI was central to the advance. 5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Compute scarcity is the new moat. Anthropic's $30B revenue and admitted capacity shortage proves that demand for frontier AI has outrun supply. The companies that locked in compute deals early — Google, Microsoft, Amazon — are now gatekeepers to who can scale.
  2. AI labs are building their own defensive coalitions. Project Glasswing, the Frontier Model Forum's anti-copying effort, and the Broadcom-Google-Anthropic TPU deal all reflect a new pattern: labs aren't just competing with each other, they're forming alliances against external threats.
  3. The model release race is approaching a new inflection point. GPT-6 arriving six days after this issue goes out. Claude Mythos 5 waiting in the wings. Gemini 3.1 Ultra already shipping. The window between "frontier" and "obsolete" is now measured in weeks.
  4. Energy efficiency could reshape the entire AI economics stack. A 100x reduction in inference energy — if it scales — changes the cost structure of every AI product. This is the breakthrough that most AI companies quietly admit is necessary for AGI-era compute to be sustainable.
  5. Quantum-AI convergence is ahead of schedule. If AI is actively accelerating quantum research, the post-quantum cryptography transition needs to happen faster than NIST's current 2030 timeline assumes. Enterprises still running RSA-2048 should be paying attention.
