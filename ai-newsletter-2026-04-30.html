Lead Story

Google's $40 Billion Anthropic Bet Ends the AI "Big Three" Era

A single investment has reshuffled the entire competitive map of artificial intelligence — leaving OpenAI isolated as former rivals become deep financial partners.

Google confirmed it will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion upfront with up to $30 billion contingent on performance milestones. The deal, first reported by TechCrunch and confirmed by CNBC, is one of the largest private-company investments in tech history. It comes on top of Amazon's existing $5 billion stake, meaning Anthropic now has two of the world's most powerful cloud providers as major financial backers.

The strategic implications are significant. Analysis from 36kr notes the deal effectively ends the AI "Big Three" era — the loosely triangular dynamic between OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind — by pulling Google and Anthropic into direct financial alignment. OpenAI, which competes directly with both, now sits without a comparable hyperscaler alliance. As part of the deal, Google Cloud is providing 5 gigawatts of compute capacity over five years, and Anthropic secured a partnership with Google and Broadcom for TPU-based computing capacity beginning in 2027.

The timing is notable: just weeks after Google co-founder Sergey Brin quietly assembled a dedicated "strike team" within Google DeepMind to close the coding gap with Anthropic, the company has now bet $40 billion on the rival it was trying to catch. In AI, the fastest path to beating a competitor is apparently buying a stake in them.

GPT-5.5 "Spud" Reclaims the Top Benchmark Spot

OpenAI dropped GPT-5.5 on April 23, internally codenamed "Spud," and it immediately shot to number one on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index with a record score of 60 — the highest any model has achieved on the benchmark. The release follows GPT-5.4's launch in March and signals OpenAI is compressing its own release cadence in response to competitive pressure from Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro, which had been leading 13 of 16 major benchmarks before Spud's arrival.

Anthropic's Mythos Is Too Dangerous to Release Publicly

Anthropic announced on April 7 that its Claude Mythos Preview — shared exclusively with roughly 50 partner organizations through Project Glasswing — will not be publicly released, citing cybersecurity risks. The reason is striking: Mythos has reportedly identified severe vulnerabilities in "every major operating system and web browser." Giving that capability to the general public, Anthropic determined, poses unacceptable risk. The decision marks a rare instance of a frontier lab voluntarily withholding a model it's already built.

Models

DeepSeek V4 Preview Closes the Gap With Frontier Labs

DeepSeek launched two preview versions of DeepSeek V4, a major update to last year's V3.2 model. TechCrunch reports the new model "closes the gap" with frontier models from OpenAI and Anthropic on key benchmarks, continuing the pattern of Chinese AI labs shipping competitive open-weight models at a fraction of the cost. DeepSeek V4 is expected to put further pressure on Western labs to justify the premium pricing of their closed models.

Infrastructure

New Research Cuts AI Energy Use by 100× Without Sacrificing Accuracy

Researchers unveiled a radically more efficient AI approach that could slash energy consumption by up to 100 times while actually improving accuracy — by combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning rather than scaling parameters alone. Separately, Google's research team unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that significantly reduces memory overhead from the KV cache, one of the biggest bottlenecks in running large language models at inference time. Both advances point toward an industry pivot from "bigger is better" to "smarter is better."

Hardware

AI Is Accelerating the Quantum Threat to Internet Encryption

New research from Google and Oratomic suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking widely-used internet encryption may arrive sooner than previously expected — and AI is helping speed the timeline. Researchers stated plainly: "There is no question that we used AI to accelerate this development." A Time analysis called the implications a scenario "the world is not prepared for." Security teams are being urged to begin post-quantum cryptography transitions now rather than waiting for quantum hardware to actually arrive.

Business

Snap Cuts 1,000 Jobs as AI Writes 65% of Its Code

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced layoffs of approximately 1,000 employees, explicitly citing "rapid advancements in artificial intelligence." AI now generates more than 65% of Snap's new code, the company disclosed — a figure that would have seemed implausible 18 months ago. The restructuring is expected to deliver over $500 million in annualized cost savings. Snap is not alone: the broader pattern of tech companies shrinking headcount while citing AI productivity gains is accelerating across the industry.

Policy

EU AI Act Deadline Holds as Trilogue Collapses Without a Deal

The European Commission, Council, and Parliament ended a 12-hour trilogue in Strasbourg on April 28 without reaching an agreement on the EU's Omnibus simplification package — but the August 2, 2026 deadline for high-risk AI system compliance remains in force. Companies operating in Europe face a shrinking runway to meet documentation, transparency, and human oversight requirements. Brussels' inability to agree on relief measures means the most stringent provisions take effect on schedule.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Big Tech is picking sides — and OpenAI is now alone. Google's $40B Anthropic investment creates a two-vs-one dynamic at the frontier. Without a hyperscaler ally of comparable scale, OpenAI faces infrastructure and distribution disadvantages that money alone may not solve.
  2. Self-imposed capability suppression is the new frontier safety signal. Anthropic choosing not to release Mythos publicly sets a precedent: the labs themselves, not regulators, may become the last line of defense against their most dangerous models.
  3. AI is already coding most of tech — and the layoffs are just starting. Snap's 65% AI-generated code stat is a leading indicator. As that number crosses 80–90% at more companies, the demand for traditional software engineering roles faces structural pressure that no retraining program can fully absorb.
  4. Efficiency, not scale, is becoming the next competitive moat. TurboQuant, 100× energy reductions, and DeepSeek V4's cost-efficiency all point the same direction: the next wave of breakthroughs will come from doing more with less, not just throwing more compute at the problem.
  5. The quantum clock is ticking — and AI just moved it forward. Post-quantum cryptography is no longer a theoretical future concern. With AI accelerating quantum hardware research, organizations that haven't started their encryption migration are already behind schedule.
