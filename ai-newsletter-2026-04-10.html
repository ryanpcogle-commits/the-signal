Lead Story

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Form an Unprecedented Alliance Against Chinese AI Theft

For the first time, the three most powerful AI labs in the world are sharing intelligence against a common adversary — and the scale of what they uncovered is staggering.

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have begun coordinating through the Frontier Model Forum to detect and block "adversarial distillation" — a technique by which Chinese competitors extract model capabilities by harvesting millions of API responses. Anthropic alone documented 16 million unauthorized exchanges from three named Chinese firms attempting to systematically reconstruct proprietary model behavior.

The arrangement is extraordinary by Silicon Valley standards. Labs that compete fiercely on model releases and revenue are now sharing threat intelligence, detection heuristics, and suspected IP addresses in near-real time. Bloomberg first reported the collaboration on April 6th; multiple sources confirmed the Frontier Model Forum is the operational hub.

The broader implication is that the model-copying threat is now large enough to override competitive instincts. If distillation at this scale goes unchecked, U.S. labs fear their years of training runs could be replicated for a fraction of the cost — erasing the compute advantage that currently defines the frontier.

Models

Claude Mythos Won't Go Public — Anthropic Locks It Behind Project Glasswing

After a data leak on March 26th confirmed the existence of Anthropic's most powerful model, Claude Mythos (reportedly 10 trillion parameters), the company announced on April 7th that it will not be publicly released. The reason: the model's cybersecurity capabilities are too dangerous to deploy broadly. Instead, Mythos is being made available exclusively to a handful of vetted partners under Project Glasswing, with the specific goal of using the model to discover zero-day vulnerabilities before rival AI systems from OpenAI and Google can be weaponized against them. It's the first time a major lab has explicitly withheld a frontier model from release on safety grounds.

Hardware

AI Helped Spark a Quantum Breakthrough — and Experts Say the World Isn't Ready

New research from Google and startup Oratomic suggests quantum computers capable of breaking standard internet encryption may arrive sooner than previously expected — and AI is directly responsible for accelerating the timeline. Researchers confirmed that AI was "instrumental" in developing the algorithm at the center of the advance, enabling in months what might have taken years through conventional methods. Time Magazine reported that cryptographers are now calling for an immediate transition to post-quantum encryption standards, warning that critical infrastructure — from banking to government communications — remains exposed.

Infrastructure

100× Greener AI: Neural-Symbolic Hybrid Slashes Energy Use Without Losing Accuracy

Researchers published findings this week showing a new architecture that combines deep neural networks with classical symbolic reasoning — cutting AI energy consumption by up to 100 times while actually improving accuracy on structured tasks. The system teaches AI agents to apply logical rules rather than rely on brute-force pattern matching, dramatically reducing the number of compute cycles needed per inference. ScienceDaily noted the approach is particularly promising for robotics, where energy constraints have been a fundamental barrier to real-world deployment at scale.

Models

Open-Weight Models Reach the Frontier: Gemma 4 and Llama 4 Maverick Arrive

Google DeepMind released Gemma 4 under an Apache 2.0 license this week — four variants optimized for coding, agentic tasks, and multimodal reasoning, running from smartphones to data centers. Simultaneously, Meta's Llama 4 Maverick landed with 400 billion parameters and a 10-million-token context window, cementing its place as the most capable open-weight model ever released. For the first time, open-weight models are genuinely competitive with closed frontier systems on most benchmarks — a milestone that fundamentally changes the economics of AI deployment for developers worldwide.

Policy

Therapy Chatbot Bans Are Spreading State by State

Maine has sent a therapy chatbot ban to the governor's desk, and Missouri is advancing a similar measure through an omnibus healthcare bill. The bills would prohibit AI systems from providing licensed mental health counseling without human oversight — a direct response to several high-profile cases in which users in crisis received inadequate or harmful AI responses. The Transparency Coalition's April 10th legislative update counts seven states now considering some form of AI mental health restriction, up from two in January.

Models

Google Quietly Ships Offline AI Dictation — No Internet Required

Google released AI Edge Eloquent on iOS this week with almost no fanfare — a dictation app powered by on-device Gemma-based speech recognition models. Once downloaded, the app works entirely offline, converting speech to text with no data sent to Google servers. The privacy-first design is a notable departure from Google's historical approach to AI features, and signals that on-device inference is now capable enough to handle real-world voice workloads without cloud connectivity.

Business

OpenAI's $25B Revenue Run Rate Has the IPO Machine Warming Up

OpenAI has surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue and is taking early steps toward a public listing as soon as late 2026, according to TechCrunch. The company's recent economic white paper — which proposed public wealth funds, robot taxes, and a four-day workweek to manage AI-driven displacement — reads partly as a policy document and partly as an IPO positioning play. Rival Anthropic, meanwhile, has pulled ahead on revenue with a $30 billion annualized run rate, having expanded its compute partnership with Google and Broadcom to access TPU capacity coming online in 2027.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Competitive labs are forming defensive coalitions. The OpenAI-Anthropic-Google alliance against adversarial distillation is a first. When the threat comes from outside the industry, the rivalry pauses. Expect this cooperation to expand into other domains — export controls, compute monitoring, watermarking.
  2. The most powerful AI models are now being kept secret. Anthropic withholding Mythos is a watershed moment. The assumption that frontier models eventually go public is no longer safe. Safety and security concerns may permanently split the AI world into "public frontier" and "classified frontier."
  3. AI is collapsing the quantum-safe encryption timeline. The Google-Oratomic breakthrough means the transition to post-quantum cryptography is no longer a 10-year project — it may be a 3-year emergency. Every organization running RSA or ECC encryption is now on a ticking clock.
  4. Open-weight models have caught the frontier. Gemma 4 and Llama 4 Maverick reaching frontier-level performance on open licenses redistributes AI power dramatically. Enterprises no longer need to pay frontier API prices — or hand their data to a third party — to access state-of-the-art intelligence.
  5. AI policy is moving from federal gridlock to state-level action. Seven states moving on therapy chatbot bans in one quarter. The U.S. federal AI governance vacuum is being filled by a patchwork of state laws — and the pace is accelerating. A compliance nightmare for AI companies is quietly taking shape.
