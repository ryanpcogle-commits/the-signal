Lead Story

The Pentagon Picked a Fight With Anthropic — and the Entire Industry Blinked

When the DOD labeled Anthropic a "supply-chain risk," it triggered a cross-company solidarity moment unlike anything the AI industry has seen.

The Department of Defense declared Anthropic a supply-chain risk this month after the AI company refused to allow its technology to be used for mass surveillance of Americans or to autonomously fire weapons. The DOD's response was swift — they immediately signed a competing deal with OpenAI.

What happened next surprised everyone. More than 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind — including Google DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean — publicly filed statements in support of Anthropic's lawsuit against the Defense Department. Rivals defending a competitor on principle is nearly unheard of in tech.

The move may have unintended consequences. Axios notes the OpenAI-DOD contract and resulting feud could inadvertently strengthen Google's position as the neutral party in government AI.

This Month in AI
Models

March Was the Most Crowded Model-Release Month Ever

Mistral Small 4 dropped March 3rd and immediately topped open-source reasoning benchmarks. Then NVIDIA unveiled Nemotron 3 Super (a 120-billion-parameter hybrid Mixture-of-Experts model) at GTC on March 11th. GPT-5.4 — with Standard, Thinking, and Pro variants — launched March 17th. Gemini 3.1 Ultra arrived March 20th with native multimodal reasoning. Grok 4.20 followed March 22nd with enhanced real-time web access. Four frontier releases in three weeks signals the competitive window is now measured in days, not quarters.

Models

Anthropic Ships Sonnet 4.6 & Opus 4.6 — With a 1M-Token Context Window

Amid the legal drama, Anthropic quietly launched Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.6 with a 1-million-token context window in beta and improved coding capabilities. Memory features also rolled out to all Claude users — the assistant now retains preferences and context across conversations for the first time.

Infrastructure

Model Context Protocol Hits 97M Installs — It's Now Just Plumbing

The Model Context Protocol crossed 97 million installs in March 2026, with every major AI provider now shipping MCP-compatible tooling. What started as an experimental standard is now foundational agentic infrastructure. Separately, Amazon and OpenAI announced a partnership to co-create a Stateful Runtime Environment on Amazon Bedrock — positioning memory and tool-use as the next architectural layer.

Video AI

OpenAI Killed Sora's API. Lightricks Shipped a Free Alternative.

OpenAI quietly wound down the Sora public API this month, citing unsustainable inference costs per generated minute. The shutdown triggered a broader industry reckoning about which video AI workloads are economically viable at scale. Meanwhile, Lightricks shipped LTX 2.3 — a 22-billion-parameter open-source video model that generates 4K video at 50 FPS with synchronized audio in a single pass, for free.

Hardware

Yann LeCun Raises $1.03B for "World Models" — Backed by NVIDIA & Bezos

LeCun's new startup, Advanced Machine Intelligence (AMI) Labs, raised a $1.03 billion seed round — one of the largest in history — backed by NVIDIA and Bezos Expeditions. The bet: the next frontier isn't bigger language models but "world models" — AI that learns by understanding the physical laws governing reality, not just statistical patterns.

Robotics

Physical AI Has Its "ChatGPT Moment"

Boston Dynamics announced it will immediately begin manufacturing a production version of Atlas — a fully electric robot that autonomously performs warehouse tasks without human assistance. NVIDIA declared at CES that "the ChatGPT moment for physical AI has arrived" and released robot-specific chips and AI models for free. The era of AI leaving the screen and entering the physical world is no longer theoretical.

Hardware

NVIDIA's Vera Rubin Platform — and Meta's Four New Chip Generations

NVIDIA unveiled Vera Rubin, its successor to the Blackwell architecture, designed for massive scaling with radical improvements in processing power and memory bandwidth. AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, and OCI will deploy Rubin-based instances in 2026. Meta separately announced four new generations of custom AI chips — MTIA 300, 400, 450, and 500 — signaling the hyperscalers are all-in on reducing dependence on NVIDIA.

Business

JPMorgan, Oracle, and Atlassian All Made the Same Bet This Month

JPMorgan Chase reclassified its massive AI investments from experimental R&D to "core infrastructure" spending. Oracle announced plans to raise up to $50 billion to fund AI infrastructure expansion. Atlassian went the other direction — laying off 1,600 employees (roughly 10% of its workforce) to redirect ~$236 million toward AI development. Three different companies, same conclusion: AI spending is no longer optional.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. AI militarization is the new flashpoint. The DOD/Anthropic conflict set a precedent: AI companies will face pressure to choose between government contracts and ethical boundaries. Every major lab now has to answer where it stands.
  2. Infrastructure spending has become existential. JPMorgan calling AI "core infrastructure." Oracle raising $50B. Atlassian firing 10% of its staff to fund AI. The companies that treat AI as optional R&D are getting left behind.
  3. Model release cadence is compressing dangerously. Four frontier models in three weeks means evaluation, red-teaming, and deployment pipelines can't keep up. The speed race is becoming a safety concern.
  4. Agentic/stateful AI is the next architectural layer. MCP at 97M installs. Amazon + OpenAI building stateful runtimes. Memory features rolling out across every major assistant. The shift from "chat" to "agent" is happening now.
  5. OpenAI is setting up its IPO run. $25B annualized revenue, early steps toward a public listing by late 2026. The company that started as a nonprofit research lab may become the most valuable tech IPO of the decade.
