Anthropic's Most Powerful Model Ever Exists — But You Can't Have It

Claude Mythos 5, a 10-trillion-parameter model, has been confirmed by Anthropic — then immediately locked away under the company's most restrictive safety classification ever.

Anthropic this week confirmed the existence of Claude Mythos 5, a model so capable that internal evaluations triggered the company's ASL-4 safety protocol — a classification reserved for AI systems approaching genuinely dangerous capability thresholds. Rather than a public release, the model is being made available exclusively to select partners under a program called Project Glasswing, with tight controls on access and use.

The disclosure comes just days after Anthropic shipped Claude Opus 4.7 on April 16 — its newest publicly available flagship, built for complex, long-horizon reasoning and extended agent workflows. Opus 4.7 represents the frontier Anthropic is willing to ship; Mythos 5 apparently represents the frontier they are not.

The decision to acknowledge and then withhold the model sets a significant industry precedent. It suggests that at least one major lab now has a working system it considers too dangerous to deploy broadly — and has chosen transparency over silence, while still keeping the keys firmly in its pocket.

Gemini 3.1 Pro Leads 13 of 16 Benchmarks — at a Third of GPT-5.4's Cost

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Pro is leading 13 of 16 major AI benchmarks and ties GPT-5.4 Pro on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, according to independent evaluations published this week. The headline number that's turning heads: Gemini 3.1 Pro delivers comparable performance at roughly one-third the API cost of its OpenAI rival. The model adds real-time voice and image analysis, and a companion Flash-Lite variant optimises for speed-sensitive enterprise workloads. Meanwhile, a Google DeepMind internal "strike team" — reportedly championed by cofounder Sergey Brin — was assembled specifically to close the gap with Anthropic's coding capabilities, suggesting that benchmark leadership and coding dominance are still two different things.

Meta Llama 4 Maverick and Google Gemma 4 Are Now the Open-Source Frontier

Meta's Llama 4 Maverick — 400 billion parameters, a 10-million-token context window — has established itself as the top open-weight model available today. Google's Gemma 4, released in four variants under the Apache 2.0 license, offers frontier-level performance to any developer willing to self-host. The simultaneous arrival of two credible open-source options at the frontier is quietly rewriting the economics of AI deployment: companies that would have paid for API access are now spinning up their own instances, and the gap between open and closed models has never been narrower.

Infrastructure

Google's TurboQuant Algorithm Cuts KV Cache Memory Overhead 6×

Presented at ICLR 2026, Google Research's TurboQuant algorithm tackles one of the most stubborn bottlenecks in running large language models at scale: the KV cache. The technique reduces memory overhead from the key-value attention cache by a factor of six, with no meaningful quality degradation on standard benchmarks. For operators running inference at scale, that translates directly into either six times more capacity on the same hardware, or a dramatic reduction in per-query infrastructure cost. The result, alongside a separate compression algorithm that reduces AI memory requirements by the same factor, signals that efficiency gains — not just raw scaling — will define the next phase of model deployment.

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Form Anti-Cloning Alliance Against China

Three of the largest AI labs in the United States — OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google — have begun sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to combat Chinese competitors extracting results from U.S. models to distill or clone proprietary capabilities. The initiative, reported by Bloomberg, represents an unusual moment of cooperation among direct commercial rivals. The threat they are uniting against: systematic "distillation attacks," in which low-cost queries to U.S. models are used to replicate their behaviour in domestically built Chinese systems without paying for training.

AI Energy Breakthrough Cuts Power Use 100× While Improving Accuracy

Researchers have unveiled an approach combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning — the kind of rule-based logic that dominated AI before deep learning — that slashes energy consumption by up to 100 times compared to standard neural network inference, while simultaneously improving accuracy on structured reasoning tasks. The work, covered by ScienceDaily, has direct implications for edge AI deployment, where power constraints currently limit what models can run locally. If the results hold at production scale, it could enable genuinely capable AI on low-power devices without cloud connectivity.

India Establishes AI Governance War Room as Job Displacement Fears Mount

The Indian government constituted the AI Governance and Economic Group (AIGEG), a high-level inter-ministerial body chaired by Union IT Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw, tasked with mapping which job profiles AI will hit first and in which geographic concentrations. The body is charged with developing mitigation and transition plans before the disruption arrives rather than after. India's move reflects a broader shift: governments that were focused on AI opportunity six months ago are now increasingly focused on AI risk — not existential risk, but the immediate, practical risk of large-scale workforce displacement without a safety net in place.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The safety ceiling is real and visible. Anthropic confirming Mythos 5 but refusing to release it is the first time a major lab has publicly drawn a line at their own product. That line will now be pressure-tested by competitors, regulators, and customers alike.
  2. Cost efficiency is becoming the competitive moat. Gemini 3.1 Pro delivering GPT-5.4-level performance at one-third the price is a direct challenge to OpenAI's revenue trajectory. The labs that win on cost per token will win enterprise contracts, even if they lose on raw benchmarks.
  3. Open-source has reached the frontier. With Meta Llama 4 Maverick and Gemma 4 both competitive at the top end, the assumption that frontier AI requires paying for API access is no longer valid. Self-hosting is back.
  4. Infrastructure efficiency is the next scaling axis. TurboQuant, energy-reduction hybrids, and compression algorithms suggest that the next performance gains won't come from bigger models but from running existing models more efficiently — on less power, less memory, and less hardware.
  5. Geopolitical AI fracture is accelerating. The U.S. lab alliance against Chinese model cloning, India's governance war room, and the DOD's ongoing AI contracting battles are all symptoms of the same dynamic: AI has become a geopolitical asset, and the rules of the road are being written in real time by whoever moves fastest.
