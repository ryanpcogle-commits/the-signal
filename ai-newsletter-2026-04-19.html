Lead Story

Anthropic Built the World's Biggest AI Model — Then Decided Not to Release It

Claude Mythos 5 has 10 trillion parameters and is restricted to a handful of vetted partners under Project Glasswing. Building it was the easy part. Deciding what to do with it is harder.

Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5 is officially the first publicly acknowledged ten-trillion-parameter AI model — engineered for high-stakes use cases in cybersecurity, academic research, and complex software development. By every available benchmark, it is the most capable language model ever built. And Anthropic has chosen not to release it to the general public.

After an early details leak on March 26 confirmed the model's existence, Anthropic announced on April 7 that Mythos 5 would remain restricted to a small group of select partners under the codename Project Glasswing. The stated reason: the model's capabilities in offensive cybersecurity are significant enough that a public release poses unacceptable risks. It's a rare case of a frontier lab voluntarily pulling back from the deployment finish line.

The decision is already shaping industry debate. If the most powerful model in existence is too dangerous to ship, what does that imply about the models that will follow? Anthropic's choice forces every other lab to answer a version of the same question — even if their answer is different.

GPT-5.4 "Thinking" Crosses Human Baseline on Desktop Task Benchmarks

OpenAI's Thinking variant of GPT-5.4 scored 75.0% on OSWorld-Verified — the most rigorous real-world desktop task benchmark available — officially surpassing the human performance baseline. The model integrates test-time compute to plan, verify, and revise multi-step actions across applications. It marks the first time a commercially available model has cleared the bar on this test, accelerating the timeline for AI that can reliably operate computers on behalf of users.

Gemini 3.1 Leads 13 of 16 Major Benchmarks — at One-Third the Price

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Ultra now leads 13 of 16 benchmarks tracked by the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, tying GPT-5.4 Pro on the composite Intelligence Index score. The cost gap is the headline: Gemini 3.1 runs at roughly one-third the API cost of GPT-5.4 Pro. For enterprise teams evaluating frontier models, that pricing differential is making Gemini the default choice for production workloads where cost matters as much as capability.

Policy

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Are Now Sharing Intelligence on China's Model Cloning

Three companies that compete fiercely for every benchmark point have started cooperating on a shared threat: Chinese competitors systematically extracting capabilities from US frontier models. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google's Alphabet are sharing information through the Frontier Model Forum to identify and disrupt distillation techniques used to clone Western models without access to their training data. It is an unusual alignment of interests among rivals — and a sign that the geopolitical dimension of AI competition is now being managed at the industry level, not just by governments.

Researchers Slash AI Energy Use by 100× — Without Sacrificing Accuracy

A team of researchers published results showing a radically more efficient inference approach that reduces AI energy consumption by up to 100 times while actually improving output accuracy on several tasks. The technique combines conventional neural network computation with symbolic reasoning — human-style rule-based logic — allowing the model to skip expensive token-by-token generation for problems with known structure. Separately, Google's TurboQuant algorithm, presented at ICLR 2026, reduces the memory overhead from KV cache by using a two-step compression process, cutting memory requirements by roughly six times.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Self-restriction is becoming a frontier lab strategy. Anthropic's Project Glasswing decision — building Mythos 5, then choosing not to release it — sets a precedent. If capability outpaces safety confidence, labs may increasingly hold models back. The question is whether market pressure makes that posture sustainable.
  2. Benchmark competition is shifting to cost efficiency. Gemini 3.1 beating GPT-5.4 on most benchmarks at one-third the price signals a new front in model competition. Raw capability scores matter less when a cheaper model can clear the bar for most enterprise tasks.
  3. Rivals are collaborating on geopolitical threats. The OpenAI-Anthropic-Google model cloning coordination is the most significant instance of frontier lab cooperation since the Frontier Model Forum launched. Expect more shared defense infrastructure as the China AI competition intensifies.
  4. The "from chat to action" transition is now measurable. GPT-5.4 Thinking clearing the human baseline on OSWorld-Verified means agentic computer use is no longer a benchmark story — it is a deployment story. Enterprise automation timelines should be revised accordingly.
  5. Quantum security risk is becoming an AI-adjacent concern. AI accelerating quantum computing breakthroughs means the two technology curves are no longer independent. Organizations that haven't started post-quantum cryptography transitions are running out of theoretical lead time.
