Lead Story

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Just Pooled $1 Billion to Stop China From Stealing Their Models

The three biggest rivals in AI have put competition aside to form a joint fund — after Anthropic alone documented 16 million unauthorized exchanges from Chinese firms systematically extracting their models.

In a striking show of industry unity, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Alphabet's Google announced the creation of the AI Frontier Fund — a $1 billion initiative aimed squarely at combating AI model extraction by Chinese companies. The fund will finance technical countermeasures, legal action, and policy lobbying to prevent what the coalition describes as systematic theft of American AI advantage.

The trigger: Anthropic disclosed it had documented 16 million unauthorized exchanges from three named Chinese firms using automated pipelines to reverse-engineer its models through the public API. The companies queried Anthropic's models at industrial scale, then used the outputs to train competing systems — a technique sometimes called "model distillation by theft." Sharing of intelligence on these actors now flows through the Frontier Model Forum, the cross-company safety body founded in 2023.

The coalition also announced that select cybersecurity partners have been given access to a restricted preview of Anthropic's unreleased Mythos model — specifically to discover zero-day vulnerabilities before bad actors can exploit them. It is an unusual move: sharing a not-yet-public model with outsiders in the name of defense.

This Week in AI
Business

Meta Unveils Muse Spark — Its First Model From the $14B Alexandr Wang Bet

Meta debuted Muse Spark, the first model out of its newly formed Meta Superintelligence Labs led by Scale AI founder Alexandr Wang, whom Meta recruited in a deal valued at roughly $14 billion. The model is already live in Meta's standalone AI app and on the web, with rollout to Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp planned in coming weeks. It marks Meta's first serious challenger to GPT-class assistants built entirely in-house since the LLaMA open-weight strategy diverged from its consumer AI ambitions.

Models

Anthropic's Claude Mythos Won't Be Released — Too Dangerous to Ship

A data leak this week confirmed the existence of Claude Mythos, Anthropic's most powerful model to date at an estimated 10 trillion parameters, with capabilities specifically tuned for advanced cybersecurity and coding. Anthropic then confirmed it on April 7 — but announced the model will not be released publicly, citing unacceptable cybersecurity risks. Access is limited to vetted partners under the codename Project Glasswing. Anthropic's annual revenue run rate has now reached $30 billion, suggesting the company can afford to keep frontier models off the market when it judges the risks too high.

Models

Gemini 3.1 Pro Leads 13 of 16 Major Benchmarks at One-Third the Cost

Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro — released in late February — has cemented its position atop the model leaderboards, scoring 77.1% on ARC-AGI-2 (a test of pure logic and novel problem-solving) and tying GPT-5.4 Pro on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index. The catch for rivals: Gemini 3.1 Pro costs roughly one-third of comparable GPT-5.4 Pro API pricing. Google also released Gemma 4, a new family of open-weight models under the Apache 2.0 license, developed by DeepMind with emphasis on coding, agentic tasks, and improved reasoning across devices.

Hardware

Intel Joins Elon Musk's Terafab — The Largest AI Chip Fab Project Ever Attempted

Intel confirmed it is joining Terafab, the semiconductor fabrication mega-project led by a consortium of Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI, with the goal of producing AI chips at a scale that has no precedent in the industry. The project aims to give Musk's AI ventures — and potentially external customers — a domestic supply of advanced chips that bypasses reliance on TSMC and existing NVIDIA supply chains. Intel's participation brings manufacturing expertise the consortium lacked; its stock surged on the announcement.

Infrastructure

Researchers Cut AI Energy Use by 100× With Neural-Symbolic Hybrid Approach

A team of researchers published results showing a radically more efficient AI architecture that slashes energy consumption by up to 100 times compared to standard deep learning — while improving accuracy on complex tasks. The system combines traditional neural networks with symbolic reasoning modules, enabling AI agents (specifically robots in lab tests) to solve novel problems through logical inference rather than brute-force pattern matching. If the results hold at scale, the approach could reshape the economics of frontier model training and inference dramatically.

Models

Meta Llama 4 Maverick: 400 Billion Parameters, 10 Million Token Context

Meta's open-weight Llama 4 Maverick model is now the largest publicly available open-source model in the world, with 400 billion parameters and a context window of 10 million tokens — enough to fit an entire codebase, legal document corpus, or research library in a single prompt. Alongside Google's Gemma 4, it marks a moment where open-weight models have effectively caught up to where proprietary models stood 12 months ago, sharply compressing the advantage of closed frontier labs.

Robotics

NVIDIA's National Robotics Week: Isaac GR00T Models Now Open

To mark National Robotics Week, NVIDIA released its Isaac GR00T foundation models as open weights — enabling robots to understand natural language instructions and execute complex multi-step tasks using vision-language-action reasoning. The release coincides with new physical AI research showing that robots trained with GR00T can generalize to novel environments without task-specific retraining. NVIDIA called it evidence that "physical AI is following the same trajectory as language AI — fast."

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. AI competition is now also a geopolitical conflict. The $1B Frontier Fund and Anthropic's 16M-exchange disclosure mark a turning point: the battle for AI leadership is no longer just between companies — it's between nations, and the labs are now openly coordinating on defense.
  2. Frontier models are being withheld for the first time. Anthropic refusing to release Mythos publicly is a new category of decision. The industry has always raced to ship. Now the most capable model in existence is being kept in a vault. Watch whether OpenAI and Google follow.
  3. Open-weight models are closing the capability gap fast. Llama 4 Maverick at 400B parameters and Gemma 4 at frontier performance under Apache 2.0 mean the "closed vs. open" advantage gap has nearly vanished. The business models of proprietary API providers face serious pressure.
  4. Energy efficiency is becoming the next moat. A 100× energy reduction would reshape training economics more than any chip release. Neural-symbolic hybrids are worth watching closely — if they scale, they could make current GPU infrastructure overkill.
  5. Meta is finally serious about consumer AI. Muse Spark and Llama 4 together represent a two-front strategy: proprietary assistant for billions of users, open weights for developers. The $14B Alexandr Wang bet suggests Meta believes AI assistants are the next platform — and it intends to own it.
