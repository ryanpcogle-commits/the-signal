Lead Story

Anthropic Doubles Its Revenue in Six Weeks — and Claude Tops Every Leaderboard

From $14 billion to $30 billion ARR since February, with more than 1,000 enterprise customers paying seven figures annually. The underdog is no longer an underdog.

Anthropic has accelerated past every revenue projection on record. The company that launched Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Opus 4.6 in February ended March at a $14 billion annualized run rate. By mid-April that number had hit $30 billion ARR — a doubling in roughly six weeks. Enterprise adoption is the engine: the company reported more than 1,000 customers now paying $1 million or more per year, up from around 500 in February.

The product story matches the revenue story. Claude Opus 4.6 this week claimed the top position on the LMSYS Chatbot Arena, surpassing GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1 Pro in overall human-preference ratings. On SWE-bench Verified — the gold-standard test for autonomous software engineering — Claude Opus 4.6 posted a record 65.3% pass rate, meaning it can independently resolve nearly two-thirds of real-world GitHub issues without human guidance.

Meanwhile, Anthropic unveiled a new model family under the working name Claude Mythos 5, described internally as a 10-trillion-parameter system targeting cybersecurity, advanced coding, and "ethical frontier" research. A mid-sized companion model called Capabara is also in testing, aimed at lower-latency enterprise deployments. No public release date has been announced, but early benchmark scores circulating in the research community suggest it outperforms every currently available model on long-horizon reasoning tasks.

Alibaba Launches "Happy Oyster" — A World Model That Builds 3D Realities

Alibaba today released Happy Oyster, a so-called world model designed to generate fully navigable 3D environments and interactive video from text prompts. Unlike conventional video diffusion models, Happy Oyster maintains physical consistency across frames — objects persist, lighting behaves correctly, and spatial relationships hold as the virtual camera moves. The company is pitching it directly at game studios, film production houses, and interactive media creators. It marks Alibaba's most ambitious push into generative world-building to date, following earlier investments in image and language models.

Gemini 3.1 Pro Tops 13 of 16 Benchmarks — Including 77.1% on ARC-AGI-2

Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro posted benchmark results this week that have rattled the research community. Its score of 77.1% on ARC-AGI-2 — more than double the 36% achieved by its predecessor, Gemini 3 Pro — represents the steepest single-generation jump in abstract reasoning performance on record. The model leads on 13 of 16 major evaluation suites. Google simultaneously made Gemini 3.1 Pro generally available on Vertex AI with enterprise features: a 2-million-token context window, document-level caching, and native video understanding at 1 frame per second for long-form analysis.

Policy

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Unite Against Chinese Model Copying

In a rare act of inter-competitor cooperation, OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have begun sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum — the nonprofit the three companies co-founded with Microsoft in 2023 — specifically to combat what researchers call "adversarial distillation": the systematic extraction of frontier-model capabilities by Chinese competitors through targeted querying that violates terms of service. The collaboration involves sharing detection signatures and blocking patterns. It is the first coordinated industry response to model-knowledge theft at scale, and signals that the labs view IP integrity as a shared interest that outweighs competitive rivalry.

AI Helped Crack Quantum Encryption's Timeline — Sooner Than Anyone Expected

New research from Google and startup Oratomic published this week suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking RSA and elliptic-curve encryption may arrive materially sooner than the 2030–2035 window most cryptographers assumed. The research team credited AI as "instrumental" in developing the Oratomic algorithm, stating there is "no question that we used AI to accelerate this development." The findings have prompted emergency briefings at NIST and within several national cybersecurity agencies. Post-quantum cryptography migration timelines, already behind schedule at most large institutions, may need to compress significantly.

Models

Meta's Llama 4 Maverick and Scout Are the New Open-Weight Benchmarks

Meta released Llama 4 Maverick and Llama 4 Scout on April 5th — both built on a Mixture of Experts architecture that delivers strong performance with significantly lower inference costs than comparable dense models. Scout is optimized for long-context retrieval tasks; Maverick targets general reasoning and code generation. Both are open-weight and available for commercial use. Early evaluations position Maverick as the strongest open-weight model for coding tasks, outperforming the previous Llama 3.3 generation by a wide margin on HumanEval and LiveCodeBench.

AI Energy Use Could Drop 100× With Symbolic-Neural Hybrid Reasoning

Researchers announced this week a technique that combines deep neural networks with classical symbolic reasoning — the kind of rule-based logic that dominated AI in the 1980s and 1990s — to produce systems that use up to 100 times less energy while achieving equal or better accuracy on structured tasks. The approach works by offloading logical inference to a symbolic layer that does not require gradient computation, reserving neural processing for perceptual and language tasks where it is irreplaceable. The implications for edge deployment and on-device AI are significant: tasks that today require cloud inference could run locally on low-power hardware within two to three years if the technique scales.

Infrastructure

Google's TurboQuant Cuts Model Memory by 6× — and MCP Crosses 97M Installs

Two infrastructure developments are reshaping how AI models are deployed at scale. Google's research team unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, a two-step compression algorithm that reduces memory overhead for large models by a factor of six with minimal accuracy loss — making frontier-scale inference economically viable on hardware that was previously insufficient. Separately, Anthropic's Model Context Protocol crossed 97 million installs in March, cementing its transition from experimental open standard to foundational agentic infrastructure. The Agentic AI Foundation, formed under the Linux Foundation in December 2025 and anchored by MCP, OpenAI's AGENTS.md, and Block's goose framework, now governs interoperability standards for the emerging agentic software layer.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Anthropic's revenue acceleration is rewriting competitive dynamics. Doubling ARR in six weeks while topping every major leaderboard is not a coincidence — it reflects a flywheel where benchmark leadership drives enterprise deals that fund the next model generation. The gap between Anthropic and the field may be widening.
  2. World models are the next frontier after language models. Alibaba's Happy Oyster, Yann LeCun's AMI Labs, and NVIDIA's physical AI push all point to the same thesis: the next leap requires AI that understands spatial and physical reality, not just token sequences.
  3. Post-quantum cryptography migration is now urgent, not optional. If AI is accelerating the timeline for cryptographically relevant quantum computers, every organization running RSA or ECC encryption needs a migration plan measured in months, not years.
  4. Open-weight models are closing the gap with closed frontier models. Llama 4 Maverick's performance on coding benchmarks confirms that open-weight MoE architectures are within striking distance of closed commercial models for most enterprise use cases — at a fraction of the inference cost.
  5. Energy efficiency is becoming a competitive moat. The 100× energy reduction from symbolic-neural hybrids, combined with TurboQuant's 6× memory compression, signals that the next wave of AI competition will be fought on inference economics, not just benchmark scores.
