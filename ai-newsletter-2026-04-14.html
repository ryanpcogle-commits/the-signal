Lead Story

GPT-6 Launches Today — and Anthropic Already Has a Model That Beats It

OpenAI's most anticipated release arrives as the AI benchmark race reaches an absurd milestone: the best publicly available models are tied, while the real frontier is locked behind closed doors.

GPT-6 goes public today, April 14 — the most anticipated model launch of the year and OpenAI's answer to months of competitive pressure from Google and Anthropic. As of last week, Gemini 3.1 Ultra and GPT-5.4 Pro sat in a dead heat at 57 points on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index — the highest publicly accessible scores ever recorded.

But here's the uncomfortable truth: Anthropic's Claude Mythos already outperforms both, and almost no one can use it. Released April 7 to just 50 organizations under the codename Project Glasswing, Mythos was developed with 10 trillion parameters and is considered too powerful — and too dangerous — for public deployment. This is the first major frontier model in history to be intentionally withheld from the public on safety grounds.

OpenAI's CRO simultaneously told staff this week that Microsoft has been limiting the company's reach to enterprise customers — while demand through Amazon Bedrock and AWS is "staggering." The most consequential partnership in tech is showing its first real cracks.

Models

Anthropic's Claude Mythos Is Real — and Off-Limits to Nearly Everyone

Anthropic's Claude Mythos, a model trained with an estimated 10 trillion parameters, quietly launched April 7 in restricted preview under Project Glasswing — a coalition of major tech and cybersecurity organizations. Analysts report Mythos has already been used to expose critical vulnerabilities in banking infrastructure. Anthropic has signaled the model will not receive a public release in 2026, citing unacceptable cybersecurity risks. It marks a watershed moment: a lab choosing deliberate restraint over competitive deployment.

Models

Meta Llama 4's 10-Million-Token Context Window Changes What "Long Context" Means

Meta's Llama 4 family — Scout (10M token context) and Maverick (1M token context) — landed April 5 as open-weight Mixture-of-Experts models. Scout's 10-million-token window is roughly 6× larger than any previously released open model and can process entire codebases, multi-year legal archives, or full research corpora in a single pass. Both models are Apache 2.0 licensed and immediately available for download. Meanwhile, Meta's first proprietary model, Muse Spark, scored 52 on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index in its debut.

Infrastructure

OpenAI vs. Microsoft: The Partnership Is Fraying in Public

OpenAI's Chief Revenue Officer told staff this week that Microsoft has structurally limited the company's ability to reach enterprise customers — a remarkable admission given how central the Azure partnership has been to OpenAI's growth. Demand through Amazon Bedrock since the late-February OpenAI-AWS partnership was described internally as "staggering." The tension signals OpenAI is actively building distribution leverage independent of its largest backer, ahead of what analysts expect to be a 2026 IPO filing.

Policy

AI Helped Break Quantum Encryption — Sooner Than Anyone Expected

New research published this week by Google and Oratomic suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking widely used internet encryption may be closer than previously modeled. Researchers at Oratomic explicitly credited AI as "instrumental" in developing the algorithm that accelerated their timeline. The findings sent shockwaves through cybersecurity circles, with Nature calling it "a real shock." Anthropic's Project Glasswing was partially formed in anticipation of exactly this kind of AI-accelerated security threat landscape.

Hardware

Microsoft's GigaTIME Turns a $10 Tissue Slide Into Advanced Cancer Imaging

Microsoft unveiled GigaTIME this week — an open-source AI system trained on 40 million cancer cells across more than 14,000 patients that generates advanced immune cell imaging from standard pathology slides costing as little as $10. Previously, comparable imaging required specialist equipment running into the hundreds of thousands of dollars. The model is freely available, and early clinical partners in the US and Europe have already begun integration testing. It may be the most practically significant open-source medical AI release to date.

Robotics

NVIDIA's National Robotics Week: Foundation Models Are Now Running Real Hardware

NVIDIA is using National Robotics Week to showcase Isaac GR00T — its new family of open robot foundation models that allow robots to understand natural-language instructions and execute complex, multi-step tasks using vision-language-action reasoning. The company demonstrated robots trained entirely in simulation deploying successfully on physical hardware with no additional tuning — a gap that previously required months of real-world data collection. Jensen Huang called 2026 "the year physical AI stops being a demo."

Infrastructure

New Architecture Cuts AI Energy Use 100× by Adding Symbolic Reasoning

Researchers published findings this month on a hybrid architecture that combines traditional neural networks with symbolic reasoning — the kind of rule-based logic humans use for structured problem-solving. The result: up to 100× reduction in energy consumption with improved accuracy on reasoning benchmarks. The approach challenges the dominant assumption that scaling compute is the only path to better AI, and arrives at a moment when the industry faces serious pressure over AI's electricity demands and grid impact.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The frontier is bifurcating. Top public models are converging at benchmark parity while restricted models like Mythos pull further ahead. The "best AI you can actually use" and the "best AI that exists" are becoming two very different things — and the gap is growing.
  2. OpenAI's Microsoft dependency is becoming a strategic liability. Describing your largest partner as limiting your growth — while publicly courting AWS — is a strategic declaration. Watch for the OpenAI-Microsoft relationship to be fundamentally restructured before any IPO filing lands.
  3. Quantum timelines just compressed, and AI did it. If AI can accelerate quantum computing research, every cybersecurity timeline needs to be revised. Encryption standards that were "safe for 10 years" may now be safe for far fewer — and governments are not ready.
  4. Medical AI is entering the open-source era. Microsoft open-sourcing a state-of-the-art cancer imaging model signals that healthcare AI is maturing from proprietary tools to public infrastructure — the same arc cloud computing took from 2008 to 2015. Expect a flood of open medical AI in the next 18 months.
  5. Energy efficiency is the new benchmark that matters. 100× energy reduction isn't a footnote — it's a potential inflection point. If symbolic-neural hybrids can match or exceed pure transformer performance at a fraction of the power cost, the economics of AI deployment change dramatically for edge, mobile, and cost-sensitive use cases.
