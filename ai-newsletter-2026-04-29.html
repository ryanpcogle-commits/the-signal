Lead Story

Google Bets $40 Billion on Anthropic — and Rewrites the Competitive Map

In the largest single investment in AI history, Google is pouring up to $40 billion into its closest competitor. The deal signals something more profound than money: the era of clean rivals is over.

Google announced it will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — starting with $10 billion immediately, with the remaining $30 billion contingent on performance milestones. Alongside the cash, Google Cloud will provide 5 gigawatts of computing capacity over the next five years, with room to scale. The deal values Anthropic at over $60 billion and gives Google deeper access to the company behind Claude, which now generates more than $30 billion in annualized revenue.

The investment is remarkable not just for its size but for what it represents. Google's own Gemini models compete directly with Claude. Sergey Brin recently assembled a dedicated "strike team" within Google DeepMind specifically to close the coding capability gap with Anthropic. Yet Google is now simultaneously funding and competing with the same company — a sign that at this scale, it's more expensive to bet against winners than to back them.

Amazon, not to be left behind, also confirmed an additional $5 billion investment in Anthropic as part of a broader agreement giving Amazon up to $100 billion in compute commitments over time. Anthropic now counts both tech giants as major investors while remaining operationally independent.

Models

April's Unprecedented Model Wave: GPT-5.5, DeepSeek V4, and Grok 4.3

The final week of April saw the densest cluster of frontier model releases in the industry's history. OpenAI reclaimed the top benchmark spot with GPT-5.5. DeepSeek fundamentally rewrote the economics of scale with DeepSeek V4 — a 1.6-trillion-parameter model that delivers frontier performance at a fraction of Western infrastructure costs. xAI's Grok 4.3 exited experimental preview and immediately secured the number-two position on the global leaderboard. Earlier this month, Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5 (10 trillion parameters, optimized for cybersecurity and coding) and Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro with real-time voice and image analysis also landed — making April the most competitive single month for frontier AI in history.

Business

Meta Debuts Muse Spark and Commits Up to $135B to AI in 2026

Meta unveiled Muse Spark, its first flagship large language model built under Chief AI Officer Alexandr Wang's newly formed Superintelligence Labs. The model delivers competitive performance on multimodal perception, reasoning, health, and agentic tasks. Alongside the launch, Meta announced AI capital expenditures of $115–135 billion for 2026 — nearly double last year's spending — making it the single largest declared AI infrastructure investment by any company this year. The message from Menlo Park: superintelligence is no longer a research aspiration, it's a budget line.

Business

Novo Nordisk Goes All-In on OpenAI Across Its Entire Business

Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk announced a sweeping strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across every part of the company — from drug discovery and clinical trials to manufacturing, supply chains, and commercial operations. Full deployment is planned by end of 2026. It is one of the most comprehensive enterprise AI commitments announced by a major non-tech company, and a signal that pharma — with its vast data, complex regulatory constraints, and enormous R&D budgets — may become one of AI's most consequential proving grounds.

Infrastructure

Researchers Cut AI Energy Use by 100x Without Sacrificing Accuracy

Scientists published a new approach that slashes AI energy consumption by up to 100 times while actually improving model accuracy. The method combines traditional neural networks with symbolic reasoning — teaching models to apply logical rules like a human expert rather than brute-forcing answers through statistical pattern matching. Early tests show robots using the system reason more reliably in novel environments and consume a fraction of the power. If it scales, it could reshape the economics of inference and reduce the industry's mounting pressure on global power grids.

Policy

Google Signs Pentagon's Classified AI Deal — Anthropic Refuses

Google has signed a classified agreement with the Pentagon allowing military use of its AI with adjustable safety filters — joining OpenAI and Elon Musk's xAI on the roster of defense AI suppliers. Anthropic declined to participate. The move mirrors last month's broader industry fracture over AI militarization: some companies are treating defense contracts as revenue, while others are treating ethical limits as competitive differentiation. The fact that Google can simultaneously invest $40 billion in Anthropic and sign a classified Pentagon deal underscores the strange geometry of this moment in AI.

Research

AI Accelerates a Quantum Breakthrough That Could Break Internet Encryption

New research from Google and startup Oratomic suggests quantum computers capable of breaking standard internet encryption may arrive sooner than previously expected — and AI was "instrumental" in developing the underlying algorithm. Researchers say AI dramatically accelerated the timeline by finding shortcuts in the mathematical search that human scientists had missed. The paper drew immediate attention from cybersecurity agencies and cryptography researchers, who note that most internet infrastructure has not yet been updated to quantum-resistant encryption standards. The World, Time magazine warns, "is not prepared."

Business

Snap Lays Off 1,000 as AI Writes 65% of Its Code

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced the layoff of approximately 1,000 employees and the closure of over 300 open roles, citing "rapid advancements in artificial intelligence" as the primary driver. Notably, Spiegel disclosed that AI now generates more than 65% of Snap's new code — one of the highest figures publicly disclosed by a major technology company. The announcement is the latest in a pattern of tech companies restructuring headcount around AI-driven productivity gains, following similar moves by Atlassian, Klarna, and others over the past year.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The "enemy investment" era has begun. Google investing $40B in Anthropic while running a strike team to beat it is not a contradiction — it's the new playbook. At frontier scale, funding your competitors is cheaper than losing to them.
  2. April became the densest AI model month ever. GPT-5.5, DeepSeek V4, Grok 4.3, Claude Mythos 5, Gemini 3.1 — all within weeks. The evaluation and safety pipeline for AI deployment simply cannot keep pace with this release cadence.
  3. Enterprise AI is moving from pilot to core infrastructure. Novo Nordisk integrating AI across every business function by year-end. Snap's 65% AI code ratio. These aren't experiments — they're structural workforce and operational changes.
  4. The Pentagon is selecting its AI vendors — and Anthropic drew a line. Google, OpenAI, and xAI are in. Anthropic is not. The defense AI market is forming now, and the ethical choices companies make in 2026 will define how they're perceived for a decade.
  5. Energy efficiency is the next compute frontier. A 100x reduction in AI energy use is not incremental — it's a potential category shift. If symbolic-neural hybrid approaches scale, the cost and carbon calculus of running AI changes entirely.
