Lead Story

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Just Formed an Anti-China IP Alliance

The three labs publicly competing for AI dominance quietly united this week to block adversarial distillation by Chinese competitors — a geopolitical shift as significant as any model release.

Bloomberg reported on April 6 that OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have coordinated through the Frontier Model Forum to jointly detect and combat adversarial distillation attacks — cases where Chinese competitors exploit API access to copy proprietary model capabilities in violation of terms of service. The move signals a new phase where intellectual property defense, not just capability competition, shapes lab strategy.

The alliance is partly a response to a pattern the labs identified over the past six months: systematically structured API queries designed to extract reasoning and knowledge representations from frontier models. Individually, none of the three companies had the telemetry to detect the full pattern. Together, they do.

The coordination is notable given the labs' fierce rivalry on benchmarks and talent. That OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google can act collectively on IP enforcement — while remaining bitter product competitors — suggests the Frontier Model Forum is quietly becoming one of the most consequential industry bodies in tech.

This Week in AI
Models

Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5 Is the First 10-Trillion-Parameter Model

Anthropic released Claude Mythos 5 this week, a 10-trillion-parameter model described internally as "a step change" beyond Opus 4.6. Initially available only to select cybersecurity and advanced coding partners, Mythos 5 targets high-stakes enterprise workloads requiring sustained multi-hour reasoning. Anthropic also released a companion accessible model called "Capabara" aimed at everyday use at lower cost. The Mythos release followed a data store misconfiguration in late March that inadvertently confirmed the model's existence — giving the announcement a rare pre-leak arc unusual for the typically tight-lipped lab.

Models

Google Cuts Veo 3.1 Prices and Launches Lite Tier — Today

Google dropped the price of Veo 3.1 Fast on April 7 and simultaneously launched Veo 3.1 Lite, a new tier that generates video from text or images at less than 50% the cost of Veo 3.1 Fast with no perceptible drop in speed. The move is a direct response to Lightricks' LTX open-source video model gaining developer mindshare, and signals Google's intent to win the video AI market on price rather than just capability. Gemini 3.1 Pro also continues to impress on benchmarks — leading 13 of 16 major evaluations and tying GPT-5.4 Pro on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index at roughly one-third the API cost.

Infrastructure

Anthropic Locks In Gigawatts of Google TPU Capacity Through 2027

Anthropic signed a landmark deal with Google and Broadcom for multiple gigawatts of TPU computing capacity set to come online starting in 2027. The agreement is one of the largest AI infrastructure commitments ever made outside of hyperscaler internal deployments — giving Anthropic dedicated silicon at a scale that could meaningfully reduce its dependence on spot compute markets. The deal reinforces the trend of AI labs locking in long-horizon infrastructure now to avoid capacity crunches as model sizes scale toward 10 trillion parameters and beyond.

Hardware

Researchers Cut AI Energy Use 100x by Fusing Neural and Symbolic Reasoning

A team of researchers published results on April 5 showing that combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning can slash AI energy consumption by up to 100x while simultaneously improving accuracy. The approach — reported by ScienceDaily — enables AI systems, including robotics agents, to "think more logically instead of relying on brute-force trial and error." The energy implications are significant: if even a fraction of inference workloads could adopt hybrid symbolic-neural architectures, data center power demand projections for 2028 and beyond would need to be substantially revised downward.

Hardware

A New Memory Chip Operates at 700°C — and Could Reshape AI Hardware

A breakthrough chip design published on April 6 can store data and perform calculations at temperatures up to 700°C (1,300°F) — roughly five times higher than the thermal ceiling of current silicon-based chips. Reported by ScienceDaily, the device uses novel materials that maintain data integrity under extreme heat, pointing toward AI accelerators that run hotter, faster, and with fewer cooling constraints. While commercial deployment is years away, the research signals a potential escape route from the thermal bottleneck that increasingly limits GPU performance density.

Business

Alibaba Pledges $100 Billion in AI and Cloud Revenue Within Five Years

Alibaba announced a commitment to reach $100 billion in combined AI and cloud revenue within five years, positioning itself as the dominant AI infrastructure provider across Asia. The pledge follows aggressive investment in its Qwen model family and cloud AI services and reflects a broader pattern of non-Western tech giants setting explicit AI revenue targets to signal strategic seriousness to investors and partners. For context, Microsoft's cloud revenue crossed $135 billion in fiscal 2025 — Alibaba is targeting direct competition at scale.

Robotics

South Korea Deploys ChatGPT-Enabled Care Robots for Its Elderly Population

South Korea is deploying thousands of ChatGPT-enabled social care robots to assist elderly citizens — the largest national-scale rollout of conversational AI in a physical caregiving context to date. The robots handle companionship, reminders, and basic health monitoring, addressing South Korea's acute demographic challenge: it has one of the world's fastest-aging populations and a severe shortage of human caregivers. The deployment offers a real-world stress test for whether AI companions can operate reliably and safely at population scale — a dataset every major robotics lab is watching closely.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Geopolitics is now an AI product decision. The Frontier Model Forum IP alliance isn't just legal defense — it's a signal that US labs are starting to coordinate on China policy the way they've resisted coordinating on anything else. Expect this to expand into export controls and API access restrictions.
  2. 10 trillion parameters changes the cost calculus entirely. Claude Mythos 5 isn't a consumer product — it's a proof that labs will sacrifice accessibility for capability at the frontier. The question now is whether 10T parameters delivers 10x the value, or just 10x the inference bill.
  3. Energy efficiency is becoming a competitive moat. The 100x neural-symbolic efficiency result, if it holds under real workloads, is as significant as any benchmark. The lab that figures out efficient reasoning first doesn't just save money — it outscales every competitor on a fixed power budget.
  4. Video AI is commoditizing faster than expected. Google dropping Veo prices on the same day a Lite tier launches suggests margin pressure from open-source (LTX) is already forcing strategic pricing. Premium video AI may have a shelf life measured in quarters, not years.
  5. Asia is writing its own AI story. Alibaba's $100B pledge and South Korea's care robot deployment aren't footnotes — they're parallel tracks. The dominant AI infrastructure narrative is still Western-lab-centric, but the deployment numbers increasingly aren't.
