Lead Story

Google Bets $40 Billion on Anthropic — and Ends the AI "Big Three" Era

A $40 billion investment in its fiercest rival signals that Google is choosing partnership over pure competition, reshaping the entire AI power map in a single announcement.

Google has agreed to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion upfront, with the remaining $30 billion tied to performance milestones — in what is being described as the largest single corporate investment in an AI lab to date. The deal values Anthropic at well above $100 billion and comes just as Anthropic's annualized recurring revenue exceeded $30 billion in March.

The move effectively dissolves the clean "OpenAI vs. Google vs. Anthropic" framing that has dominated AI coverage for two years. With Google now deeply entangled in Anthropic's success, analysts at 36kr describe the shift as the end of the AI "Big Three" era — leaving OpenAI, which is separately eyeing a public listing after surpassing $25 billion in annualized revenue, increasingly isolated as the lone fully independent frontier lab.

Inside Google DeepMind, co-founder Sergey Brin has simultaneously assembled a dedicated strike team of researchers and engineers tasked specifically with closing the gap between Google's coding AI and Anthropic's Claude — the very product Google just bet $40 billion on supporting. The strategic contradiction is not lost on observers.

DeepSeek V4 Arrives at 1.6 Trillion Parameters — The Largest Model Yet

China's DeepSeek released DeepSeek V4 this week, a 1.6-trillion-parameter model that represents the largest publicly disclosed language model by parameter count. The release follows DeepSeek's pattern of catching up to — or surpassing — Western frontier models at a fraction of the reported training cost. V4 arrives alongside OpenAI's GPT-5.5 and xAI's Grok 4.3, which also shipped in the same late-April wave of releases, making the past ten days arguably the most competitive model-release stretch of 2026.

Meta Cuts 8,000 Jobs While Promising to Spend Up to $135 Billion on AI

Meta announced roughly 8,000 layoffs on the same day it revealed AI capital expenditure guidance of $115 to $135 billion for 2026 — nearly double the company's AI spending last year. Microsoft also announced buyouts covering approximately 8,750 US employees, about 7 percent of its American workforce. The twin announcements illustrate what is becoming a defining pattern of the AI era: headcount contracting and infrastructure investment expanding in lockstep, with human labor costs explicitly redirected toward compute.

Anthropic Built a 10-Trillion-Parameter Model — and Decided Not to Release It

Claude Mythos 5, Anthropic's flagship model with an estimated 10 trillion parameters, was confirmed this month — but will not be released publicly. Under the codename Project Glasswing, Anthropic determined that Mythos 5's capabilities in advanced cybersecurity and autonomous coding pose risks that outweigh the benefits of open access. Access is being extended only to select partners under strict oversight. It is the first time a major frontier lab has completed a flagship model and proactively withheld it from release on safety grounds — a significant precedent regardless of where one stands on AI risk.

Gemini 3.1 Ultra Ships With a 2-Million-Token Context Window

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Ultra is now available with a 2-million-token context window that processes text, images, audio, and video natively in a single pass — no transcription step required. The Ultra tier also adds real-time voice and image analysis, while Gemini 3.1 Pro is currently tied with GPT-5.4 for first place on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index. At Google's Next conference in Las Vegas this week, the company also unveiled a suite of agentic AI tools designed to help enterprises deploy, orchestrate, and monitor AI agents at scale across their internal software environments.

Infrastructure

Google's TurboQuant Cuts AI Memory Overhead by 6× at ICLR 2026

Google's research team presented TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, an algorithm targeting the KV cache — the memory bottleneck that limits how many simultaneous user sessions a large model can handle. Using a two-stage compression method combining PolarQuant vector rotation and Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss projection, TurboQuant reduces KV cache memory requirements by approximately six times with minimal accuracy loss. If it ships at production scale, the economics of running frontier models improve dramatically — fewer GPUs required per user served.

Business

Novo Nordisk Is Handing Its Entire Business to OpenAI

Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk — maker of Ozempic and Wegovy — announced a sweeping strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across every business function: drug discovery, clinical trial design, manufacturing, supply chains, and commercial operations. Full deployment is planned by the end of 2026. If it works as described, Novo Nordisk would become the most comprehensively AI-integrated large company in the world. It is also the clearest signal yet that Fortune 500 companies are moving from AI pilots to AI-first operating models.

AI Helped Crack a Quantum Encryption Algorithm — Sooner Than Anyone Expected

New research from Google and startup Oratomic suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking standard internet encryption could arrive sooner than previously forecast — and AI was central to the acceleration. The Oratomic team stated that AI was "instrumental" in developing their algorithm, compressing what might have been years of human-only research into months. The findings prompted renewed urgency in government and enterprise circles around post-quantum cryptography standards, which NIST finalized in 2024 but most organizations have yet to implement.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The "Big Three" framing is dead. Google investing $40 billion in Anthropic ends the clean rivalry narrative. The new map is OpenAI (independent, IPO-bound) vs. a Google-Anthropic alliance vs. Meta (going it alone with massive capex). That's a fundamentally different competitive landscape.
  2. AI-driven layoffs and AI investment are the same announcement now. Meta and Microsoft cut thousands of jobs and raised AI spending in the same breath. This template — human headcount down, compute capex up — will be copied across the Fortune 500 throughout 2026.
  3. The most powerful model ever built isn't for sale. Anthropic completing Mythos 5 and withholding it on safety grounds sets a precedent every lab will now be measured against. The question of what to do with a model that's "too capable" just became a real governance problem.
  4. Context windows have outrun most use cases. Two million tokens means Gemini 3.1 Ultra can ingest entire codebases, multi-year document archives, or hours of audio in a single call. The bottleneck is no longer context length — it's workflow design and retrieval quality.
  5. Quantum + AI is the threat combination that keeps security teams up at night. AI accelerating quantum research means the timeline for encryption-breaking quantum computers just got shorter and harder to estimate. Organizations still treating post-quantum migration as a "2030 problem" need to revisit that assumption now.
