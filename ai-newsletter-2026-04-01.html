OpenAI Raises $122 Billion — and Starts Talking About Going Public

The largest private fundraise in technology history closed March 31st, pushing OpenAI past $25 billion in annualized revenue and setting the stage for a potential IPO by late 2026.

OpenAI closed a $122 billion funding round on March 31, 2026 — dwarfing its previous record round and cementing it as the most capitalized private company in the world. The round was led by a consortium of sovereign wealth funds and institutional investors, with no single lead publicly named. At $25 billion in annualized revenue — up from roughly $3.4 billion just 18 months ago — OpenAI is now generating more than most Fortune 500 companies.

Sources at the company have confirmed early-stage conversations with investment banks about a public listing as soon as late 2026. The structure would convert the company's capped-profit LLC into a standard public benefit corporation, a move the board has been deliberating since 2024. CEO Sam Altman declined to comment publicly but told employees the IPO timeline is "real and accelerating."

Rival Anthropic is approaching $19 billion in annualized revenue — remarkable in its own right, but now clearly second in the commercial race. Meanwhile, global VC investment hit a record $297 billion in Q1 2026, up 150% year-over-year, with AI startups capturing 81 cents of every venture dollar invested.

Anthropic's "Claude Mythos" Leaked — Described as Their Most Powerful Model Ever

Internal documents leaked on March 26th confirm Anthropic has completed training on a model codenamed Capybara, to be released publicly as Claude Mythos. The documents describe it as "by far the most powerful AI model we have ever developed" — positioned above the existing Opus tier in an entirely new capability class. With 10 trillion parameters, Mythos is being trialed first with cybersecurity partners. No public release date has been announced, but early access customers have reportedly been onboarded as of this week.

Gemini 3.1 Pro Leads 13 of 16 Major Benchmarks — at One-Third the Cost of GPT-5.4

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Pro is now the top-ranked model on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, tying GPT-5.4 Pro on overall capability while costing roughly one-third the API price. It scores 77.1% on ARC-AGI-2, 94.3% on GPQA Diamond, and features a 1-million-token context window with native multimodal reasoning across text, images, audio, video, and code. Google also shipped Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused variant delivering 2.5x faster response times at just $0.25 per million input tokens — aggressively undercutting the entire field on price-performance.

GPT-5.4 "Thinking" Scores Above Human Expert Level on Economic Tasks

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Thinking model scored 83.0% on the GDPVal benchmark — a test designed to measure performance on tasks with real economic value, scored against verified human domain experts. For context, the human expert baseline sits at 79.2%. On OSWorld-V, which simulates real desktop productivity tasks, GPT-5.4 scored 75%, edging above the 72.4% human baseline. These are the first published benchmark results where an AI model has demonstrably exceeded human expert performance on economically meaningful work.

Apple Picks Google Gemini for Siri — Samsung Bringing AI to 800 Million Devices

Apple announced it is transforming Siri with "on-screen awareness" and cross-app integration, partnering with Google to run its 1.2-trillion-parameter Gemini model on Apple's Private Cloud Compute infrastructure. The deal is notable for what it isn't: a reliance on OpenAI. Separately, Samsung committed to equipping 800 million devices with Google's Gemini AI by the end of 2026, bringing generative AI to mid-tier and budget smartphones globally — not just flagship hardware.

Meta Llama 4 Maverick: 400B Parameters, 10M Context Window — Free to Run

Meta's Llama 4 Maverick has emerged as the strongest open-weight model available, with 400 billion parameters and a 10-million-token context window. It runs free on your own infrastructure and is now matching commercial frontier models on several coding and reasoning benchmarks. The open-source parity story — dismissed as wishful thinking a year ago — is now a credible operational reality for enterprise teams that can run their own hardware.

Perplexity AI Faces Class-Action Suit Over Data Sharing With Meta and Google

Perplexity AI is being sued in a class-action lawsuit alleging it shared personal user data from chat conversations with Meta and Google — without disclosure or consent. The lawsuit is the first major legal action targeting an AI search product's data practices specifically. Separately, the EU has barred its press teams from using fully AI-generated content, and a new Quinnipiac poll found more than half of U.S. adults believe AI is likely to cause societal harm — the highest reading since the survey began in 2023.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. AI has crossed the human expert threshold on economic work. GPT-5.4 Thinking at 83% on GDPVal isn't a benchmark curiosity — it's the first documented case of AI outperforming verified human domain experts on tasks with real dollar value attached. This is the threshold executives have been waiting to act on.
  2. The model tier above Opus now exists. Claude Mythos's leaked existence signals that "frontier" is no longer a fixed ceiling. Labs are building a new class of model above their current best — and the first deployment is in cybersecurity, not consumer chat.
  3. Open-source parity is operational, not theoretical. Llama 4 Maverick at 400B parameters running free on-premise is now a genuine alternative to commercial APIs for enterprises with infrastructure budgets. The leverage OpenAI and Anthropic hold on pricing is narrowing.
  4. AI privacy is becoming the next regulatory battleground. The Perplexity lawsuit, EU content bans, and public trust erosion are arriving at the same time. The next 12 months will determine whether AI companies face data-handling rules as strict as financial services.
  5. The OpenAI IPO will reshape the entire industry's incentive structure. A public OpenAI changes everything: analyst coverage, quarterly pressure, governance scrutiny, and competitor positioning. Every major lab is now implicitly planning for a world where the market sets OpenAI's agenda.
