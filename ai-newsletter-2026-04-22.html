Lead Story

Anthropic Built the Most Capable AI Model Ever — and Decided You Can't Have It

Claude Mythos scored 93.9% on SWE-bench Verified and 94.6% on GPQA Diamond. It will not be released to the public. The age of truly frontier-locked AI has begun.

On April 7, Anthropic confirmed the existence of Claude Mythos — a 10-trillion-parameter model that represents the most capable AI system the company has ever built. Its benchmark scores are unlike anything previously disclosed: 93.9% on SWE-bench Verified (software engineering tasks requiring real coding) and 94.6% on GPQA Diamond (graduate-level scientific reasoning). For context, the best publicly available models score in the low-to-mid 60s on SWE-bench.

The catch: Anthropic has no plans to release Mythos publicly. The company cited safety concerns around a model of this capability level, describing it as "research infrastructure" rather than a product. Alongside Mythos, Anthropic also unveiled a mid-range model called Capabara — its publicly available counterpart — while Claude Opus 4.6 has simultaneously climbed to the top of the LMSYS Chatbot Arena leaderboard, surpassing GPT-5.4 and Gemini 3.1 Pro.

The decision to vault the most capable model while shipping a competitive mid-tier option marks a new strategic posture for Anthropic: maintaining an internal frontier far ahead of what competitors can benchmark against. It's a meaningful shift from the labs' previous "ship when ready" culture — and it's forcing other labs to reconsider what they disclose about their own internal capability ceilings.

This Week in AI
Models

Llama 4's Mixture-of-Experts Architecture Breaks the Open-Source Context Record

Meta's Llama 4 Scout and Maverick, released April 5, introduced the first Mixture-of-Experts architecture in the Llama family — with Scout featuring 17 billion active parameters and a 10-million-token context window, the longest native context of any open-source model ever shipped. Maverick handles heavier reasoning workloads. Both models are available for download and commercial use, and immediately became the strongest open-weight options at their respective size classes on most major benchmarks.

Models

Google Gemma 4 Arrives Fully Multimodal — Coding Scores Jump from 110 to 2,150

Google's Gemma 4 family launched April 2 in four variants spanning 2.3B to 31B parameters, all natively multimodal from the ground up. The headline figure: the 31B Dense model's Codeforces ELO rating jumped from 110 in Gemma 3 to 2,150 in Gemma 4 — an almost unimaginable improvement in competitive programming capability. The 31B model also ranked third globally on Arena AI among all open models. The full Gemma 4 family runs across devices from smartphones to data centers, with Google emphasizing agentic and coding use cases.

Policy

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Form a United Front Against Chinese Model Copying

Rivals OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google have begun coordinating through the Frontier Model Forum to combat Chinese competitors extracting results from cutting-edge US AI models. Bloomberg first reported the collaboration on April 6, describing the companies sharing intelligence about distillation and extraction attacks used to clone frontier capabilities without access to the underlying weights. The cooperation marks a rare moment of industry solidarity — three companies that compete fiercely for the same customers aligning on a shared threat they've concluded no single lab can address alone.

Infrastructure

MCP Hits 97 Million Installs — the Agentic Standard Is Now Just Infrastructure

Anthropic's Model Context Protocol crossed 97 million installs in March 2026, and every major AI provider now ships MCP-compatible tooling. What launched as an experimental open protocol is now the de facto interface layer connecting AI agents to the tools, files, and APIs they need. Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro also went generally available on Vertex AI this week with enterprise-grade 2-million token context, document-level caching, and improved grounding with Google Search — making MCP-connected enterprise deployments significantly more viable at scale.

Hardware

Researchers Slash AI Energy Use by 100× by Teaching Models to Reason Like Humans

A research team published results this month showing a hybrid approach — combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning — that reduces AI energy consumption by up to 100 times while improving accuracy on robotics tasks. Rather than brute-force gradient descent, the system applies human-style logical inference to problems that don't require probabilistic guessing. The finding arrives at a critical moment: inference costs are the primary constraint on AI deployment at scale, and a 100x efficiency gain would reshape the economics of the entire industry if it generalizes beyond the lab setting.

Robotics

NVIDIA's National Robotics Week: Physical AI Is Moving From Simulation to Factory Floor

NVIDIA marked National Robotics Week by highlighting a wave of breakthroughs in robot learning, simulation, and foundation models that are accelerating the gap between virtual training and real-world deployment. Robots trained in NVIDIA Isaac Sim are now transferring skills to physical hardware faster than any previous generation — a milestone the company describes as "sim-to-real closing." Separately, Apple officially announced a completely reimagined AI-native Siri for 2026 with cross-app integration, powered in part by Google's Gemini model — a sign that even Apple is pragmatically sourcing external AI rather than relying solely on in-house development.

Business

Snap Cuts 1,000 Jobs Citing AI; Novo Nordisk Bets Its Drug Pipeline on OpenAI

Snap announced layoffs of approximately 1,000 employees and closure of over 300 open roles, with CEO Evan Spiegel citing rapid AI advancements as the primary driver — and projecting over $500 million in annualized cost savings by the second half of 2026. On the other end of the spectrum, Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk announced a full-company partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across drug discovery, manufacturing, and supply chains, with deployment planned by end of 2026. The two announcements capture the dual reality of the moment: AI is simultaneously eliminating roles and becoming the core bet entire industries are making on their futures.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The frontier is being locked away. Anthropic's decision to build and withhold Mythos signals a new dynamic: the most capable models may never ship publicly. Other labs almost certainly have internal models they aren't disclosing either. The public leaderboard is no longer the real leaderboard.
  2. Open-source is narrowing the gap — fast. Llama 4's 10-million token context and Gemma 4's coding leap prove the open-weight tier is no longer a lagging indicator. Enterprises that bet on open models in 2025 are looking smarter by the week.
  3. US AI labs are crystallizing a "we vs. China" posture. The Frontier Model Forum coordination on model extraction attacks is the clearest sign yet that competitive rivalry is being subordinated to geopolitical alignment when the stakes are high enough.
  4. Energy efficiency is the next performance benchmark. A 100x inference efficiency gain matters more than a 5% benchmark improvement when inference costs are the primary bottleneck to deployment. Watch for efficiency metrics to start appearing alongside accuracy numbers in model cards.
  5. Every industry is now picking its AI partner. Novo Nordisk + OpenAI. Apple + Google Gemini. The era of "we're evaluating options" is over. Companies are committing to platforms, and the lock-in implications are significant for both sides.
