Dario Amodei Called Sam Altman's Statements "Straight Up Lies" — and the Industry Chose Sides

Google is quietly moving into the vacuum. Alphabet is set to provide AI agents to the DoD's 3-million-person workforce for unclassified work — a deal the company describes as "immaterial" relative to its $400B+ annual revenue base.

Alibaba's Qwen 3.5 Outscored GPT-OSS-120B on Harvard-MIT Math — With a 9B Model

Alibaba's Qwen team released four dense models (0.8B, 2B, 4B, and 9B parameters) under Apache 2.0 — all natively multimodal with no separate vision adapter and capable of analyzing videos up to two hours long. The benchmark results were striking: the 9B model scored 83.2 on the Harvard-MIT math competition benchmark vs. GPT-OSS-120B's 76.7, and 81.7 on GPQA Diamond vs. GPT-OSS-120B's 71.5. A 9-billion-parameter model outperforming a 120-billion- parameter model on graduate-level reasoning is the kind of efficiency gain that reshapes deployment economics.

AlphaEvolve Has Been Quietly Recovering Google's Computing Resources for Over a Year

Google DeepMind revealed that AlphaEvolve — a Gemini-powered coding agent that pairs large language models with evolutionary algorithms — has been deployed silently inside Google's own infrastructure for over 12 months. Apple confirmed a partnership with Google to power a completely reimagined Siri using Gemini's 1.2-trillion-parameter model, debuting in 2026. 5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. The AI industry's ethical divide is now public and permanent. Amodei calling Altman's statements lies. OpenAI engineers resigning. Rivals filing amicus briefs. The question of military AI use has fractured the industry in a way that won't quietly heal.
  2. Efficiency is beating scale on benchmarks. Alibaba's 9B Qwen model outperforming a 120B model on graduate math is not a fluke — it's a structural shift. Inference economics will increasingly favor smaller, smarter models over larger, costlier ones.
  3. AI is becoming invisible infrastructure inside big tech. AlphaEvolve running silently inside Google for a year — recovering compute, optimizing kernels — is a preview of how AI will operate: not as a chatbot, but as a continuous optimization process running underneath everything.
  4. Conversational commerce is arriving faster than expected. Shopify's Agentic Storefronts and the Apple-Google Siri deal both point to the same future: AI assistants as the primary interface between consumers and the commercial web.
  5. The recursive self-improvement clock is ticking. Morgan Stanley citing 2027 as a possible date for recursive self-improvement loops is not fringe analysis — it's a major bank's published research. Whether the timeline is right or not, the question is no longer hypothetical.
