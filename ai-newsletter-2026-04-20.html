Lead Story

Anthropic Ships Claude Opus 4.7 — and Introduces the Most Restricted Model in AI History

Anthropic launched its most capable public model while quietly deploying a 10-trillion-parameter system so powerful it's available to just 50 organizations worldwide.

Claude Opus 4.7, released April 16, 2026, is Anthropic's most powerful publicly available AI model to date — leading every other model on the hardest coding benchmarks. The launch came alongside an unusual disclosure: Anthropic's other April release, Claude Mythos, a 10-trillion-parameter system previewed under the codename Project Glasswing, is available to only 50 hand-selected organizations while Anthropic works through its safety implications.

The Mythos numbers are striking: 93.9% on SWE-bench Verified and 94.6% on GPQA Diamond — benchmarks where GPT-5.4 Pro and Gemini 3.1 Ultra are tied at 57 points. The restricted rollout marks the first time a major AI lab has publicly acknowledged deploying a system it considers too capable for unrestricted release. Anthropic issued a cautionary note on April 10 calling Mythos "a remarkable step forward" while raising explicit concerns about broad deployment.

For the vast majority of developers, Opus 4.7 is the relevant release — and it's formidable in its own right. Anthropic's pricing collapse continues: frontier-level performance is now available at a fraction of 2024 costs, a trend reshaping the economics of every AI-powered product.

This Week in AI
Models

Google Gemma 4 Runs Frontier-Class Performance on a Single GPU

Google DeepMind released Gemma 4 on April 2 under the Apache 2.0 license — four open-source models ranging from 1B to 27B parameters, including a 27B multimodal variant that handles text, images, and video inputs. The headline: all four models run on a single 80GB NVIDIA H100 GPU while delivering benchmark performance comparable to models 20 times their size. The release cements the pattern that open-source is no longer a consolation prize — it's a legitimate choice for serious production workloads.

Hardware

New Architecture Cuts AI Energy Use by 100× — Without Sacrificing Accuracy

Researchers published a paper this week describing a hybrid approach that slashes AI energy consumption by up to 100 times while improving accuracy by combining neural networks with symbolic reasoning — the kind of rule-based logic humans use instinctively. Current large models burn energy proportional to parameter count; the new architecture decouples capability from compute cost in ways the field hasn't seen before. If it scales, it could fundamentally change the economics of inference and make AI viable in low-power edge devices.

Policy

OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google Form Anti-Copying Coalition Against Chinese AI

The three frontier AI labs announced in early April that they are sharing intelligence through the Frontier Model Forum to combat Chinese competitors systematically extracting results from US AI models. The practice — sometimes called "model distillation at scale" — allows a well-resourced actor to close capability gaps without funding foundational research. The coalition is an unusual moment of industry cooperation between companies that compete fiercely on every other dimension. Specific countermeasures have not been disclosed publicly.

Infrastructure

Google's TurboQuant Solves One of the Biggest Bottlenecks in Running Large Models

Google Research unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026 this month — an algorithm that dramatically reduces the memory overhead caused by the KV cache, the data structure that lets language models remember earlier parts of a conversation. In practice, the KV cache forces a hard tradeoff between context length and GPU memory cost. TurboQuant cuts that memory requirement by a factor of six without meaningful quality loss, which means longer contexts, lower inference bills, and more headroom for longer agentic task runs.

Hardware

AI Helps Crack Quantum Encryption Timeline — Sooner Than Anyone Expected

New research from Google and Oratomic suggests that quantum computers capable of breaking standard internet encryption may arrive significantly sooner than previous estimates. The Oratomic team reported that AI was "instrumental" in developing their new algorithm — with one researcher stating "there is no question that we used AI to accelerate this development." The finding has immediate policy implications: organizations relying on RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography may have less runway than previously assumed to migrate to post-quantum standards. NIST finalized its post-quantum cryptography standards last year, but enterprise adoption remains slow.

Robotics

NVIDIA's GR00T Models Enable Robots to Follow Natural Language in the Real World

During National Robotics Week, NVIDIA released new open Isaac GR00T foundation models that allow robots to understand natural language instructions and perform complex, multi-step tasks using vision-language-action reasoning. The models are trained in simulation and transfer directly to physical hardware — closing the sim-to-real gap that has historically been the primary barrier to deploying robots outside of tightly controlled environments. Several manufacturing and logistics companies are already piloting the technology.

OpenAI surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue this month, and the company is reportedly taking early steps toward a public listing potentially as soon as late 2026. 5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Restricted-access frontier models are becoming standard practice. Claude Mythos available to 50 orgs. GPT-5.4 Pro in limited preview. The frontier is now a two-tier system: a public model and a more capable model that most people can't access. That gap will widen before it narrows.
  2. AI and quantum computing are converging on a hard deadline. AI is accelerating quantum algorithm development. Quantum computing is accelerating AI chip design. The feedback loop is tightening — and the organizations that haven't started post-quantum cryptography migration are now behind schedule.
  3. Open-source has closed the capability gap with proprietary models. Gemma 4's 27B model delivering frontier-comparable results on a single GPU is a structural shift — not a one-off. The era of "open-source is three years behind" is over.
  4. Energy efficiency is the next moat in AI infrastructure. The 100× energy reduction paper signals where the next research arms race is headed. As inference scales, electricity costs are becoming a strategic differentiator — not just an operational concern.
  5. Frontier labs are learning to cooperate on defense, compete on offense. The OpenAI/Anthropic/Google anti-copying coalition is the second major instance of cross-lab cooperation in 2026. Expect more — geopolitical pressure creates strange alliances.
