Google Bets $40 Billion on Anthropic — and Half Its Profit Already Depends on the Outcome

The largest private AI investment in history turns Google into both Anthropic's biggest backer and its fiercest competitor. The line between ally and rival has never been blurrier.

Google confirmed this week it will invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic, providing a combination of cash and cloud computing resources. The deal dwarfs every prior AI investment on record and cements Anthropic's position as the most heavily capitalised safety-focused AI lab in the world — despite the company being excluded from the Pentagon's AI contracts just days earlier.

The financial stakes for Alphabet are already staggering. When Alphabet reported a record $62.6 billion quarterly profit, analysts noted that roughly $28.7 billion — nearly half — came not from advertising or cloud services but from marking up the value of equity it holds in private companies, primarily Anthropic. The company's profit is now materially dependent on a startup it did not create and does not control.

The arrangement creates a structural tension that will define the next era of AI competition. Google is simultaneously building Gemini to compete directly with Claude, running the cloud infrastructure Anthropic trains on, and holding equity whose value rises every time Anthropic wins a customer. No one in tech has navigated a dependency quite like this before.

GPT-5.5 Ships Six Weeks After GPT-5.4 — Agentic Coding Leaps Forward

OpenAI released GPT-5.5 on April 23, just six weeks after GPT-5.4, marking the fastest back-to-back frontier model release the company has ever shipped. The new model delivers major gains in agentic coding, computer use, and extended knowledge-work tasks. GPT-5.5 Pro is expected to land in the API imminently. OpenAI's annualized revenue has now crossed $25 billion, and the company is reportedly taking early steps toward a public listing as soon as late 2026.

Gemini 3.1 Ultra: 2-Million Token Context, True Native Multimodal

Google's most significant model launch of the year arrived this week. Gemini 3.1 Ultra processes up to 2 million tokens natively across text, image, audio, and video — without transcription intermediaries — a first for any commercially available frontier model. Alongside it, Google released Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused variant delivering 2.5× faster response times and 45% faster output at just $0.25 per million input tokens, undercutting nearly every comparable model on price.

Pentagon Clears 7 AI Firms for Classified Networks — Anthropic Absent

The Department of Defense struck agreements with Amazon Web Services, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, OpenAI, SpaceX, and Reflection — later adding Oracle — to deploy AI on its classified networks. Anthropic was conspicuously absent, having been effectively blacklisted after insisting on safety guardrails that would prevent autonomous weapons use. The White House has since reopened discussions with Anthropic following the company's announcement of Mythos, a cybersecurity tool capable of identifying advanced threats — bringing Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei to a meeting with Chief of Staff Susie Wiles.

Models

Zhipu AI's GLM-4.7 Posts the Lowest Hallucination Rate of Any Frontier Model

China's Zhipu AI released GLM-4.7 this week, a model notable for two reasons: it was trained entirely on Huawei Ascend silicon — not a single NVIDIA GPU — and it recorded a 1.2% hallucination rate, the lowest self-reported figure from any frontier lab to date. The result demonstrates that China's domestic AI stack is maturing faster than most Western analysts anticipated, and that factual reliability, not raw benchmark scores, may be the next axis of competition.

Infrastructure

New Symbolic-Neural Hybrid Cuts AI Energy Use by 100× While Improving Accuracy

Researchers published results this week for a hybrid architecture that combines traditional neural networks with symbolic reasoning — the kind of rule-based logic humans use to navigate unfamiliar situations. The system, tested on robotic navigation tasks, reduced energy consumption by up to 100 times compared to pure neural approaches while simultaneously improving accuracy. If the results replicate at scale, it could reshape economics at every layer of the AI stack, from inference chips to data-center power contracts.

Hardware

Huawei's AI Chip Revenue on Track for $12 Billion — Up 60% Year on Year

Huawei expects its AI chip division to generate roughly $12 billion in revenue in 2026, up 60% from $7.5 billion last year, driven by surging orders for its Ascend 950PR accelerator. The growth underscores how US export restrictions have inadvertently accelerated China's domestic semiconductor ambitions — creating a parallel AI hardware ecosystem that is increasingly self-sufficient. GLM-4.7's Huawei-only training stack is the clearest proof yet that the strategy is working.

SpaceX Acquires xAI, Folding Grok Into Musk's Wider Empire

SpaceX announced the acquisition of xAI, the AI lab Elon Musk founded in 2023, bringing Grok and its underlying infrastructure under the SpaceX corporate umbrella alongside Tesla and Starlink. The deal consolidates Musk's AI assets into a single entity with unique access to satellite connectivity, automotive sensor data, and aerospace compute. xAI's most recent product, Grok Imagine 1.0, a fully functional AI video generation platform, launched just days before the acquisition closed.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Investor and competitor are becoming the same entity. Google's $40B Anthropic bet — while building Gemini — is without precedent. When half your profit comes from a rival's valuation, the incentives are impossible to untangle cleanly.
  2. China's AI stack is achieving self-sufficiency. GLM-4.7 trained entirely on Huawei silicon. Ascend chip revenue up 60%. US export controls were designed to slow this — the evidence suggests they accelerated it instead.
  3. Model release cadence is now measured in weeks, not quarters. GPT-5.5 arrived six weeks after GPT-5.4. Anthropic shipped four major Claude updates in 50 days. Evaluation, safety red-teaming, and enterprise deployment pipelines simply cannot keep pace.
  4. Pentagon AI deals are the new loyalty test. Being included — or excluded — from DoD contracts now defines a lab's relationship with the US government. Anthropic's safety-guardrail stance cost it a contract and earned it a blacklist. That calculus will force every lab to decide where it stands.
  5. Energy efficiency is the next frontier arms race. A 100× energy reduction from hybrid symbolic-neural AI, if it scales, would redistribute power away from whoever can afford the biggest data centers. The era of brute-force compute dominance may have a shorter runway than it looks.
