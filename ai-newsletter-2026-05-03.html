Lead Story

Google Bets $40 Billion on Anthropic — While Signing the Pentagon Deals Anthropic Refused

The search giant's record investment in Anthropic values the safety-focused lab at $380 billion, even as Google simultaneously expands its own military AI contracts that Anthropic declined on principle.

Google announced on April 24 it would invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — with an initial $10 billion tranche at a $380 billion valuation and up to $30 billion more contingent on performance milestones. The deal makes Google the largest external backer of the company founded by former OpenAI researchers Dario Amodei and Daniela Amodei.

The investment's timing is striking. One week earlier, the Pentagon awarded classified-network AI contracts to seven firms — OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon Web Services, Nvidia, SpaceX, and startup Reflection AI — conspicuously leaving Anthropic off the list. Anthropic had been excluded after refusing to remove safety guardrails on military use of its AI, a position that cost it the contract but apparently not Google's confidence.

The move underscores a strange new dynamic in AI: the biggest investors are hedging across competitors, ideologies, and even conflicting positions on government use of AI. Google is simultaneously Anthropic's largest backer and a Pentagon supplier doing the work Anthropic won't do. For Anthropic, the $40 billion vote of confidence suggests its safety stance is a strategic asset, not just an ethical one.

This Week in AI
Models

GPT-5.5 Ships — Six Weeks After GPT-5.4

OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.5 Pro on April 23, just six weeks after the GPT-5.4 launch. The new models are rolling out to Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise subscribers in ChatGPT and Codex. The cadence is a signal in itself: OpenAI appears to be shipping incremental improvements on a roughly monthly cycle rather than waiting for major architectural leaps. Anthropic, meanwhile, has shipped four significant Claude updates in roughly 50 days during early 2026, matching the pace.

Business

Anthropic's Revenue Tripled in Four Months

Anthropic's annualized revenue run rate topped $30 billion this week, up from $9 billion at the end of 2025. The number of enterprise customers spending $1 million or more per year doubled from 500 to 1,000 in roughly two months. The company's refusal to take Pentagon contracts without safety guardrails has not slowed commercial adoption — if anything, the principled stance appears to be a selling point for regulated industries like finance, healthcare, and legal services wary of liability from unconstrained AI.

Policy

Pentagon Picks Seven AI Vendors for Classified Networks — Anthropic Is Notably Absent

The Department of Defense cleared OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon Web Services, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI to deploy their AI on its classified networks, per Breaking Defense. Anthropic was excluded after insisting on safety guardrails governing autonomous weapons use — a condition the Trump administration rejected. Google's inclusion drew immediate internal backlash: 950 employees have signed an open letter against Pentagon AI deployments without Anthropic-style safeguards, and a senior Google DeepMind research scientist publicly called the deal "deeply troubling." The White House has since reopened discussions with Anthropic.

Models

Gemini 3.1 Ultra Arrives With a 2-Million-Token Context Window

Google released Gemini 3.1 Ultra with a 2-million-token context window — double the previous record — that works natively across text, image, audio, and video in a single pass. The model is now integrating into millions of vehicles via Android Auto, giving drivers a conversational AI interface without a phone. Separately, Mistral AI shipped a new 128-billion-parameter flagship model alongside an agentic "Work mode" in Le Chat that can execute multi-step tasks asynchronously in the cloud.

Business

Novo Nordisk Partners With OpenAI to Automate Drug Discovery End-to-End

Danish pharmaceutical giant Novo Nordisk — maker of Ozempic and Wegovy — announced a strategic partnership with OpenAI to integrate AI across its entire business: drug discovery, clinical trials, manufacturing, supply chain, and commercial operations, with full deployment planned by end of 2026. The deal is one of the largest and most comprehensive AI deployments in life sciences to date. If it works at scale, it could compress the average 12-year drug development timeline by years and establish a template other pharma giants will follow.

Policy

NIST Evaluates DeepSeek V4 Pro: Cost-Efficient, But 8 Months Behind U.S. Leaders

The Center for AI Standards and Innovation at NIST published its May 2026 evaluation of DeepSeek V4 Pro, finding the open-weight Chinese model performs comparably to GPT-5 on most benchmarks but trails the leading U.S. closed models by roughly eight months of capability development. On cost efficiency, however, DeepSeek V4 outperformed GPT-5.4 mini on five of seven benchmarks. The evaluation adds empirical weight to the argument that open-weight models from China are catching up on cost-effectiveness faster than on raw capability.

Infrastructure

Back-Office AI Agents Move From Pilot to Production Across Enterprise

Salesforce, Workday, and a cluster of startups are now shipping production-grade AI agents that autonomously handle vendor onboarding, payroll reconciliation, expense routing, and contract review — routing only genuine exceptions to humans. The shift from chatbot to autonomous back-office operator is happening faster than most enterprises planned for. SHRM's State of AI in HR 2026 report puts the data in context: AI use across HR tasks hit 43 percent this year, up from just 26 percent in 2024 — a near-doubling in 18 months.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Safety is becoming a competitive moat, not just ethics. Anthropic's refusal to accept Pentagon contracts without guardrails cost it a government deal but earned it a $40B Google investment and enterprise trust that's driving revenue to $30B annualized. Principled positions are monetizable when your customers are regulated industries.
  2. Model release cycles have collapsed to weeks. GPT-5.4 to GPT-5.5 in six weeks. Four Claude updates in 50 days. The evaluation and red-teaming pipelines needed to safely deploy these models weren't built for this cadence.
  3. Big Tech is betting on everyone at once. Google invests $40B in Anthropic while simultaneously expanding Pentagon AI that Anthropic refused. Microsoft backs OpenAI while selling Azure to everyone else. The hyperscalers are no longer picking sides — they're buying stakes in the entire field.
  4. The open-weight cost curve is squeezing closed models. DeepSeek V4 beating GPT-5.4 mini on cost in five of seven benchmarks is the tell. The capability gap between open and closed models is narrowing while the price gap is widening in open's favor. Closed-model pricing pressure is coming.
  5. Enterprise AI has crossed the "core infrastructure" threshold. $700B in Big Tech AI infrastructure spend. Novo Nordisk deploying AI across its entire business. SHRM reporting 43% AI adoption in HR. The era of AI pilots is over — the era of AI as operating system for the enterprise has begun.
