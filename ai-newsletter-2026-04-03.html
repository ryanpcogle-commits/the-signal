Lead Story

Google Ships Gemma 4 Under Apache 2.0 — Open-Weights AI Just Lost Its Last Restrictions

With four model variants, full multimodal capability, and zero usage limits, Gemma 4 is the most permissive open-weight release from a frontier lab to date. The open-source AI landscape just changed.

Google DeepMind released Gemma 4 on April 2–3, a family of four open-weight models built from the same research base as Gemini 3. For the first time, Google chose an Apache 2.0 license — no monthly active user caps, no acceptable-use policy enforcement, no restrictions on commercial or sovereign deployments. Developers can download, modify, and ship the models however they choose.

The lineup spans four sizes: E2B and E4B (compact, using novel "Per-Layer Embeddings" that feed secondary signals into every decoder layer), a 26B Mixture-of-Experts model (3.8B active parameters across 128 experts), and a 31B Dense flagship that Google claims outperforms competitors twenty times its size. All four variants are multimodal by default, supporting image understanding, video comprehension up to 60 seconds, and audio recognition. Context windows reach 256K tokens on the larger models.

Day-one framework support landed simultaneously: Hugging Face Transformers, vLLM, llama.cpp, MLX for Apple Silicon, LM Studio, and even transformers.js for in-browser inference. The release is a direct challenge to Meta's Llama series dominance in open-weights — and with Apache 2.0 licensing, it removes the last argument for why Llama's terms were a dealbreaker.

Microsoft Releases Three MAI Models — A Direct Shot at OpenAI and Google

Microsoft unveiled MAI-Transcribe-1, MAI-Voice-1, and MAI-Image-2 through Azure Foundry and a new US-only MAI Playground — the company's most significant move yet toward its own foundation model stack. MAI-Transcribe-1 covers 25 languages, ranks #1 by FLEURS benchmark in 11 of the top 25 global languages, runs at ~50% lower GPU cost than leading alternatives, and is priced at $0.36 per hour. MAI-Voice-1 generates 60 seconds of expressive speech in under one second on a single GPU from just a few seconds of voice sample. MAI-Image-2 debuted at #3 on the Arena.ai image leaderboard. All three already power Copilot, Bing, PowerPoint, and Azure Speech.

Anthropic Researchers Find 171 "Emotion-Like" States Inside Claude — and They Causally Matter

Published April 3, Anthropic's interpretability team released research showing that Claude Sonnet 4.5 contains 171 internal representations that function analogously to human emotions — and that these patterns causally influence the model's decisions, not merely correlate with its outputs. The finding is a landmark in AI interpretability: prior work could identify emotion-like activations but couldn't prove they affected behavior. This research does. The implications for AI safety and alignment are significant — if models have functional emotions, questions about wellbeing and manipulation become technically meaningful, not just philosophical.

Cognichip Raises $60M to Use AI to Design AI Chips — Promising 75%+ Cost Reduction

Startup Cognichip raised $60 million to apply AI to semiconductor design, claiming it can cut chip development costs by more than 75% and halve development timelines. The thesis is a compounding one: faster, cheaper chips accelerate future AI hardware, which in turn makes AI-assisted chip design even more capable. If the claims hold at scale, it creates a feedback loop that could meaningfully shift the economics of the entire AI compute stack — with implications for NVIDIA's stranglehold on the market.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Apache 2.0 is the new open-weights standard. Gemma 4's fully permissive license removes the last argument against open-weight models in enterprise and sovereign deployments. Expect Meta to face pressure to match it in Llama 5.
  2. Microsoft is decoupling from OpenAI. Three proprietary MAI models in one day signals that Microsoft is building a parallel model stack. The OpenAI partnership is becoming more competitive than complementary.
  3. AI is designing its own hardware. Cognichip's $60M raise is part of a broader trend — AI-assisted EDA (electronic design automation) promises to collapse semiconductor development timelines. The compute bottleneck may be self-solving.
  4. Interpretability is becoming a safety instrument. Anthropic's 171 emotional states finding isn't just academic — it's evidence that black-box AI is becoming less opaque. Causal interpretability tools are moving from research curiosity to alignment infrastructure.
  5. China's release cadence is now matching the US. Three Alibaba models in days. GLM-5. Qwen open-weights. The assumption that Western labs have a sustained capability lead is increasingly hard to defend on publicly available evidence.
