Lead Story

Anthropic Drops a 10-Trillion-Parameter Model — and the Scale Race Enters Uncharted Territory

Claude Mythos 5 is officially the first widely recognized 10-trillion-parameter AI model. It didn't arrive quietly — a data leak confirmed its existence weeks before the announcement.

Anthropic launched Claude Mythos 5 in early access this week, making it available first to cybersecurity partners and academic research institutions. The model is engineered specifically for high-stakes environments — complex coding, adversarial security research, and scientific analysis — rather than general consumer use. A data leak had already confirmed Anthropic was training the model before the public announcement, building anticipation for weeks.

The jump to 10 trillion parameters — roughly 10x the estimated size of GPT-4 — represents a qualitative shift in what the industry believes is feasible to train and serve. If Mythos 5 follows the same path as Claude's previous releases, a broader rollout will come in the weeks ahead.

The timing is notable: Anthropic is simultaneously fighting a Department of Defense lawsuit over its refusal to allow its technology for classified military applications — and winning the PR battle. Claude jumped to the number-one spot on the US App Store this week as backlash against OpenAI's Pentagon deal intensified.

#QuitGPT: OpenAI's Pentagon Deal Triggers a User Revolt

After OpenAI agreed to deploy its AI on classified US Department of Defense networks, the backlash was immediate and massive. The #QuitGPT movement attracted over 2.5 million supporters, and ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% overnight. Anthropic had refused the same deal on ethical grounds — and benefited directly, with Claude reaching the number-one spot on the US App Store for the first time. OpenAI and Google employees have publicly signed letters defending Anthropic in the resulting DOD lawsuit, in a cross-company solidarity moment the industry has rarely seen.

SpaceX Acquires xAI for $250 Billion

SpaceX completed its acquisition of Elon Musk's AI company xAI in a deal valued at $250 billion — one of the largest technology acquisitions in history. The deal consolidates Musk's AI ambitions under the SpaceX umbrella and raises immediate questions about xAI's Grok models, the future of its X platform integration, and how a privately held space company becomes one of the world's most powerful AI players. Regulatory review timelines remain unclear.

Models

GPT-5.4 "Thinking" Scores 83% on the GDPVal Benchmark — Human Expert Level

OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Thinking variant scored 83.0% on GDPVal, a benchmark measuring performance on economically valuable expert tasks. The score places the model at or above human expert level. GPT-5.4 also set records on computer-use benchmarks OSWorld-Verified and WebArena Verified, demonstrating autonomous multi-step execution across software environments. The model is available in Standard, Thinking, and Pro variants, with Pro surpassing human-level performance on desktop task benchmarks and a 1-million-token context window across the line.

Infrastructure

Google's TurboQuant Cuts AI Memory Needs by 6x

Google unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026, a new quantization algorithm that reduces AI memory requirements by 6x using a combination of PolarQuant and the Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss algorithm. The breakthrough directly addresses the Key-Value cache bottleneck that has made running large models in data centers increasingly expensive. If widely adopted, TurboQuant could meaningfully lower the infrastructure costs of frontier model inference — relevant for every lab currently building at scale.

Gemini 3.1 Ties GPT-5.4 Pro — at One-Third the API Cost

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.1 Pro leads 13 of 16 major AI benchmarks and ties GPT-5.4 Pro on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index — at roughly one-third of the API cost. The Flash-Lite variant delivers 2.5x faster response times and 45% faster output, priced at just $0.25 per million input tokens. Meanwhile, Google also released Gemma 4 under an Apache 2.0 open-source license — the first Gemma generation with OSI-approved terms, signaling a more aggressive open-source push from DeepMind.

Models

Open-Source Surges: Llama 4 Maverick Hits 10M-Token Context, Qwen 3.6-Plus Ships

Meta's Llama 4 Maverick — a 400-billion-parameter open-weight model with a 10-million-token context window — has quickly become the strongest open-weight model available, narrowing the gap with closed frontier labs. Separately, Alibaba released Qwen 3.6-Plus on April 2nd, with marked improvements in automated coding and AI-agent capabilities. The open-source tier is no longer meaningfully behind the frontier.

Hardware

Meta's MTIA Chip Rollout and Nvidia's 400 Gbps Silicon Photonics Deal

Meta announced deployment of its MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) chips across its data centers, with MTIA 450 and 500 slated for mass deployment by 2027 — part of the hyperscaler push to reduce NVIDIA dependence. On the interconnect side, Coherent Corp expanded its supply agreement with NVIDIA following a breakthrough in 400 Gbps silicon photonics, accelerating data transfer within AI clusters. Faster interconnects mean larger models can be served more efficiently — a critical bottleneck at the 10-trillion-parameter scale Anthropic just reached.

Half of Planned US Datacenter Builds Are Stalled

A shortage of key electrical distribution components has caused approximately half of all planned US datacenter builds to be delayed or canceled. The bottleneck — not chips, not land, not permits, but transformers and switchgear — reflects the infrastructure reality of the AI boom. Every frontier lab is racing to secure compute, but the physical grid is struggling to keep up with demand that outpaced years of utility planning.

5 Patterns Worth Watching

  1. Ethics is becoming a competitive advantage. Anthropic's refusal of the DOD deal sent Claude to #1 on the App Store while triggering a 2.5M-person revolt against OpenAI. Users are now making AI choices on ethical grounds — not just capability. That's new.
  2. The parameter ceiling just moved dramatically. Claude Mythos 5 at 10 trillion parameters signals that the scale race hasn't plateaued. The question now is whether raw scale still translates to capability gains — or whether efficiency breakthroughs like TurboQuant matter more.
  3. Open-source has caught up to 2024's frontier. Llama 4 Maverick with a 10M-token context window and Qwen 3.6-Plus shipping in April means the open-weight tier now competes seriously with where GPT-4 was a year ago. The closed-model moat is shrinking.
  4. Consolidation is accelerating. SpaceX acquiring xAI for $250B is the biggest signal yet that the AI industry is moving toward consolidation. Fewer, larger players — each with hardware, models, data, and distribution — will define the next phase.
  5. Infrastructure is the binding constraint now. Half of US datacenter builds stalled. Transformers and switchgear shortages. Nvidia signing photonics deals to speed up cluster interconnects. The models exist — the physical infrastructure to serve them at scale is the hard problem.
